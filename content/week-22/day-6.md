# Week 22, Day 6

## 👶 Baby Development
As you enter Shabbat, your baby is nestled comfortably, weighing about 15 ounces and measuring around 11 inches. The skin is becoming more opaque as layers develop, though it's still quite thin. Blood vessels are visible beneath the surface, giving the skin a reddish appearance. The hair on your baby's head is growing, though it has no pigment yet — it's bright white, regardless of what color it'll eventually be. Your baby continues to swallow amniotic fluid, and the kidneys are producing urine that contributes back to the fluid. The whole system is a beautifully efficient cycle.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Friday can bring a sense of relief as the workweek ends. Your partner's body has been working overtime — growing a baby while also working, maintaining daily life, and dealing with all the physical changes. She might be extra tired today. The uterus is now about an inch above the belly button, and it's probably visible through most clothing. She might be enjoying maternity clothes or finding creative ways to extend her regular wardrobe. Comfort is key.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Shabbat Chicken with Hidden Veggies:** Season chicken thighs with paprika, garlic powder, cumin, and salt. Place in a baking dish on a bed of sliced onions, quartered zucchini, sweet potato chunks, and cherry tomatoes. Drizzle with olive oil, roast at 200°C (400°F) for 40 minutes. The chicken provides protein and iron, and the vegetables roast in the chicken juices, making them incredibly flavorful. One pan, minimal cleanup — perfect for Erev Shabbat.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Today's movement is restorative. **Legs Up the Wall** is a pregnancy-safe inversion that relieves swollen feet and legs:
1. Sit sideways next to a wall
2. Swing legs up the wall as you lie back
3. Scoot hips close to (but not touching) the wall
4. Rest arms by sides, palms up
5. Stay for 5-10 minutes, breathing deeply

This helps reduce swelling, relieves lower back tension, and calms the nervous system. Perfect Friday afternoon reset.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
**Erev Shabbat task takeover:** Make Friday evenings easier for your partner by taking on the dinner prep, table setting, and cleanup. Even if you normally share these duties, she's carrying extra weight (literally) and the end of the week hits harder during pregnancy. This isn't about keeping score — it's about noticing where you can lighten her load and just doing it, without being asked.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's hair is currently white because it lacks melanin (the pigment that gives hair its color). Melanin production kicks in later in pregnancy. Some babies are born with a full head of dark hair; others arrive nearly bald. And the hair your baby is born with often falls out and grows back a completely different color or texture. You truly won't know what you're getting until it grows in!

## 💰 Gear Tip: The Car Seat
It's not too early to start researching car seats. In Israel, a car seat is required by law from birth, and you literally cannot take the baby home from the hospital without one. Here's what to know:
- **Group 0+** seats are rear-facing and suitable from birth to about 13 kg
- **ISOFIX** base systems are easiest to install correctly (reduces human error)
- Don't buy second-hand unless you know its full history — car seats expire and should be replaced after any accident
- Practice installing it before the baby arrives. It's trickier than it looks
- Budget range: 500-1500 NIS for a good infant seat

Start looking now so you're not rushing later. Read reviews, ask parent friends, and check Israeli safety ratings.

## 🧘 Mindfulness Moment
Before Shabbat begins, try this grounding exercise together:
1. Each of you places one hand on the baby belly
2. Close your eyes
3. Take 3 deep breaths, syncing your breathing
4. On each inhale, silently name something you're looking forward to
5. On each exhale, release one worry from the week
6. Open your eyes, make eye contact, and say one kind thing to each other

This takes two minutes and sets a beautiful tone for the evening.

## 🕯️ Shabbat Activity
**Memory Lane Walk:** After dinner, take a slow walk together (weather permitting) and share childhood Shabbat memories. What did Shabbat look like in your family? What did you love about it? What would you change? This conversation naturally leads to imagining what Shabbat will look like for your growing family. If you don't have Shabbat traditions from childhood, this is your chance to create them from scratch — which is its own kind of beautiful.
