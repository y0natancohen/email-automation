# Week 22, Day 4

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's eyes are fully formed now — but there's a twist. The iris (the colored part) has no pigment yet. Eye color is determined by genetics and won't be finalized until months after birth. In fact, many babies are born with blue or gray eyes that later change color. Behind those colorless irises, the retina is developing the layers it needs to eventually process light. The eyebrows and eyelashes are also in place, making your baby's face incredibly expressive, even with eyes still closed.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 22 weeks, your partner might be experiencing what's sometimes called "the waddle" — a shift in walking pattern as the pelvis loosens and the belly grows. This is caused by the hormone relaxin, which softens ligaments to prepare for birth. It can make her hips feel looser and her gait wider. She might also notice occasional numbness or tingling in her hands, especially at night — this could be carpal tunnel syndrome, which is surprisingly common during pregnancy due to fluid retention putting pressure on nerves.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Vitamin A for eye development (Full Recipe): Sweet Potato & Chickpea Curry**

Your baby's eyes are developing rapidly and need vitamin A.

*Ingredients:*
- 2 sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 can chickpeas, drained
- 1 can coconut milk
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp curry powder
- 1 tsp ginger (fresh or ground)
- Handful of spinach
- Rice for serving

*Directions:*
1. Sauté onion and garlic in coconut oil (5 min)
2. Add curry powder and ginger, stir 1 minute
3. Add sweet potatoes and chickpeas, stir to coat
4. Pour in coconut milk plus 1/2 cup water
5. Simmer 20 minutes until potatoes are tender
6. Stir in spinach until wilted
7. Serve over rice

Sweet potatoes are loaded with beta-carotene (which converts to vitamin A), and the coconut milk provides healthy fats for nutrient absorption.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Arm Circles & Upper Body Mobility:**
1. Stand with arms extended out to the sides
2. Make small circles forward — 15 times
3. Small circles backward — 15 times
4. Increase to large circles — 10 each direction
5. Finish by clasping hands behind the back and gently stretching the chest open

This helps counteract the shoulder rounding that happens as the belly grows and the chest gets heavier.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Baby eye color is determined by multiple genes, not just one. Two blue-eyed parents can have a brown-eyed baby (though it's less common). Most babies of European descent are born with blue or gray eyes that may darken over the first year. Babies with more melanin (common in Middle Eastern, African, and Asian heritage) are often born with brown eyes that stay brown. Your baby's final eye color might not be settled until they're about 3 years old!

## 🌍 Cultural & Science Deep-Dive: How Babies Learn Touch in the Womb
The sense of touch is actually the first sense to develop — it begins as early as 8 weeks. By week 22, your baby's entire body is sensitive to touch. Researchers have used 4D ultrasound to observe fascinating behaviors: babies touch their faces 50+ times per hour, grab their feet, and explore the uterine wall. This self-touch isn't random — it's how the brain maps the body. Each touch teaches the brain, "This is my hand, this is my face, this is where I end and the world begins." In developmental psychology, this process is called body schema formation, and it's the earliest form of self-awareness. When your partner feels the baby moving against her belly, and she presses back, and the baby responds — that's a genuine interaction between two conscious beings.
