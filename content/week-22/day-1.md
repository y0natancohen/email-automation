# Week 22, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to Week 22! Your baby is now about 11 inches long and weighs around 15 ounces — roughly the size of a papaya. This week marks a visible transformation: your baby is starting to look like a miniature newborn. Lips and eyelids are becoming more distinct, and the face is filling out. The sense of touch is developing rapidly, and your baby is exploring everything within reach — face, feet, umbilical cord. That grip is getting strong enough to grab and hold the cord (don't worry, this is perfectly safe and normal). The lungs are developing but aren't yet mature — that's a process that continues well into the third trimester. This week, we'll focus on your partner's changing body, body image during pregnancy, and how to be the supportive partner she needs right now.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is starting to look like the person you'll meet in a few months. The lips are more defined, giving the face a recognizable shape. Eyelids are fully formed, though still fused shut. The eyes are developing behind those closed lids, though the iris doesn't have pigment yet — eye color comes later. Your baby's skin is still wrinkled because there's not enough fat underneath yet, but that will change in the coming weeks. Touch is the hot development this week — your baby is constantly feeling, grabbing, and exploring.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 22 weeks, the belly is clearly visible, and your partner might be navigating mixed feelings about her changing body. Some women love their pregnancy shape; others struggle with it. Both reactions are valid. Physical symptoms this week might include continued round ligament pain, back aches, and possibly hemorrhoids (increased blood flow and the pressure of the uterus can cause them). She might also notice increased vaginal discharge and more frequent Braxton Hicks contractions.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Protein-Packed Shakshuka (Full Recipe)**

Your baby needs protein for all that growth. This Middle Eastern staple is perfect.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 can crushed tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, 1/2 tsp chili flakes
- 4-5 eggs
- Fresh cilantro or parsley
- Crumbled feta (optional)
- Crusty bread for serving

*Directions:*
1. Sauté onion and pepper in olive oil until soft (5 min)
2. Add garlic and spices, cook 1 minute
3. Pour in tomatoes, simmer 10 minutes until thickened
4. Make wells in the sauce, crack eggs into them
5. Cover and cook on medium-low until eggs are set (7-8 min)
6. Top with herbs and feta, serve with bread

Each egg delivers about 6g of protein plus choline, which is essential for baby's brain development.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Wall Push-Ups:**
1. Stand arm's length from a wall, hands flat on the wall at shoulder height
2. Lean forward, bending elbows, keeping body straight
3. Push back to starting position
4. Do 3 sets of 10

Wall push-ups are a safe way to maintain upper body strength, which you'll need for all the baby-carrying ahead. They also help with posture as the belly grows.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Body image during pregnancy is a sensitive topic. Your partner's body is doing something incredible, but she might not always see it that way. Be genuine and specific with compliments. Don't say "you don't even look pregnant" (this dismisses her experience). Instead, try "I love watching our baby grow" or "you're beautiful, and I'm in awe of what your body is doing." If she's struggling, listen without trying to fix. Your consistent appreciation matters more than you know.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one thing I do that makes you feel most supported right now? And is there something I could do better?"

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's grip is now strong enough to grab and hold the umbilical cord. This isn't dangerous — the cord is slippery and tough, and the baby is just practicing motor skills. Think of it as your baby's first toy.
