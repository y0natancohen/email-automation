# Week 34, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's immune system is getting a boost right now. Antibodies are crossing the placenta from your partner to the baby, helping build up immunity that will protect your little one in the first weeks after birth. This passive immunity is one of nature's most clever designs — baby essentially "borrows" mom's immune defenses until their own system is ready to take over.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Sleep is becoming a real challenge. Between the belly size, the need to pee every hour, leg cramps, and difficulty finding a comfortable position, a full night's rest feels like a distant memory. Hip pain is common as the ligaments loosen in preparation for birth. Your partner might also notice increased vaginal discharge — this is normal as the body prepares for labor.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Quick Iron-Rich Recipe: One-Pan Lentil & Spinach Bowl**

- 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 1 can diced tomatoes
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tsp cumin
- Squeeze of lemon juice
- Salt to taste

Sauté garlic for 1 minute, add lentils, tomatoes, cumin, and 2 cups water. Simmer 15-20 minutes until lentils are soft. Stir in spinach until wilted. Squeeze lemon on top. Serves 2. Packed with iron, folate, and fiber — all crucial right now.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
If your partner is experiencing hip pain at night, gentle hip circles during the day can help. Standing with feet hip-width apart, slowly circle the hips like using a hula hoop — 10 circles in each direction. Cat-cow stretches on all fours are also fantastic for relieving lower back pressure and keeping the spine mobile.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's something nobody tells you: you should start sleeping more now, too. Seriously. Bank rest while you can. Once baby arrives, you'll be running on less sleep than you thought possible. If you can, start going to bed 30 minutes earlier this week. Your future self will thank you.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one thing from your own childhood that you definitely want to do with our baby? And one thing you'd do differently?" This can spark a really meaningful conversation about your parenting values.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby has taste buds and can actually taste what your partner eats through the amniotic fluid. Studies suggest that babies exposed to certain flavors in the womb are more likely to accept those flavors after birth. So if your partner is eating lots of garlic and spices, your baby might end up being an adventurous eater!

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
**The Pillow Fort Strategy:** Your partner needs a pregnancy pillow if she doesn't have one already. If a full-body pillow isn't available, here's the arrangement: one pillow between the knees, one supporting the belly, one behind the back, and the regular head pillow. Sleeping on the left side is generally recommended as it improves blood flow to the uterus and baby. Keep a water bottle by the bed to avoid full trips to the kitchen.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If your partner is still working, now is the time to finalize her maternity leave handover plan. Key tasks, passwords, ongoing projects, and contact info should all be documented. For you, start having conversations with your employer about your paternity leave plans. Even if it feels early, having these arrangements confirmed reduces stress enormously. Make sure you know exactly how many days you're taking and when your leave starts — you don't want to be sorting this out while rushing to the hospital.
