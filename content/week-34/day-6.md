# Week 34, Day 6

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's adrenal glands are producing hormones — specifically, they're making about ten times more cortisol than an adult's adrenal glands (relative to body size). These hormones play a crucial role in lung maturation and will help trigger the cascade of events that starts labor when the time is right. Baby's body is essentially sending signals that say, "I'm almost ready."

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Nesting instinct might be kicking in hard. Your partner may suddenly feel the urge to deep-clean the house, organize the nursery for the third time, or fold baby clothes at 11 PM. This is a real biological drive, not just being particular. She might also be experiencing more lower back pain as the baby's weight shifts forward. Constipation and hemorrhoids are common and uncomfortable — encourage fiber, water, and movement.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Quick Calcium-Rich Recipe: Tahini Banana Smoothie**

- 1 banana
- 2 tbsp tahini
- 1 cup milk (dairy or fortified plant-based)
- 1 tbsp honey
- Pinch of cinnamon
- Handful of ice

Blend until smooth. This provides a great calcium, magnesium, and potassium boost. The tahini adds healthy fats and a nutty flavor. Takes 2 minutes and counts as a snack and drink in one.

**Dark Chocolate Almond Energy Bites (No-Bake)**

Your baby's CNS is in overdrive this week, and mom needs sustained energy to keep up. These no-bake bites are rich in magnesium (from almonds and dark chocolate) and provide a quick pick-me-up without a sugar crash.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 cup almond butter
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/3 cup dark chocolate chips (70% cacao or higher)
- 2 tbsp ground flaxseed
- 2 tbsp maple syrup
- 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
- Pinch of sea salt

*Directions:*
1. Combine almond butter, oats, ground flaxseed, maple syrup, and salt in a mixing bowl. Stir until well combined.
2. Fold in the dark chocolate chips and shredded coconut.
3. Refrigerate the mixture for 15 minutes so it firms up slightly.
4. Roll into 14-16 small balls using damp hands.
5. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week, or freeze for longer.

Almonds and dark chocolate are two of the best dietary sources of magnesium, which helps reduce leg cramps, supports restful sleep, and aids baby's nervous system development. Keep a few in your hospital bag for snacking during labor.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Encourage your partner to practice labor positions today: hands and knees, slow dancing (leaning on you for support), sitting backward on a chair, and gentle bouncing on a birth ball. These positions can help baby move into an optimal position for birth and give her a sense of what feels comfortable. Practice them together — you'll be her support during labor.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's your complete hospital bag checklist — save this:

**Your bag:**
- Phone charger and portable battery pack
- Snacks (granola bars, nuts, dried fruit, chocolate)
- Water bottle
- Change of clothes (2 sets — labor can be messy)
- Toiletries (toothbrush, deodorant, face wash)
- Pillow from home (put a colored pillowcase so it doesn't get mixed up)
- Entertainment (book, tablet, headphones — for downtime)
- Cash/coins for vending machines
- Comfortable shoes (you'll be on your feet)
- A small Bluetooth speaker for music (if your partner wants it)

**Her bag:**
- Comfortable robe and nightgown (that opens in front for skin-to-skin)
- Non-slip socks or slippers
- Lip balm (hospitals are dry)
- Hair ties and headband
- Nursing bra and breast pads
- Maternity underwear or disposable ones
- Toiletries and face wipes
- Going-home outfit (still maternity-sized — the belly doesn't disappear instantly)
- Phone charger

**Baby's bag:**
- Going-home outfit (bring 2 sizes — newborn and 0-3 months)
- Swaddle blanket
- Car seat (installed and checked before you go!)
- Hat and socks
- Diapers and wipes (hospital usually provides, but backup is smart)

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What music should we have ready for the delivery room?" Making a playlist together can be a fun, low-pressure activity. Think calming tracks for early labor, maybe something more energizing for pushing, and a special song for when baby arrives.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies in the womb hiccup — a lot. You might see rhythmic, repetitive movements in your partner's belly that look different from kicks. These hiccups are actually baby's diaphragm practicing for breathing. They're totally normal and can happen several times a day.

## 💰 Financial/Gear Tip
**The Car Seat — Do NOT Wait on This.** If you haven't bought and installed your infant car seat yet, this is the week. Most hospitals will not let you leave without a properly installed car seat. Here's what to know:
- Rear-facing only for infants (this is the law and the safest position)
- Install it in the center of the back seat if possible
- Many fire stations and police stations offer free car seat inspections — take advantage of this
- Read the manual cover to cover (each seat is different)
- Practice buckling a teddy bear or doll to get the hang of the harness
- The chest clip should be at armpit level
