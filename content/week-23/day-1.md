# Week 23, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to Week 23! Your baby is about 11.4 inches long and weighs approximately 1 pound. This week brings wonderful developments: your baby's face is fully formed and looking more and more like the person you'll meet. The skin is developing more layers and gaining pigment, though it's still wrinkled and translucent. Your baby can hear loud sounds and may startle in response — conversations, music, and your heartbeat are all part of their world now. The lungs are continuing to develop their branching airways, and the early foundations for surfactant production are beginning to take shape. Blood vessels in the lungs are also forming to prepare for gas exchange after birth.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's face is now so well-formed that if you could take a photo, you'd see a tiny version of the person you'll meet in a few months. The eyelids are still fused shut (they'll open around week 26), but the eyebrows are becoming visible. Some researchers believe babies at this stage can already distinguish between light and dark through the closed eyelids.

## 👶 Baby Development
At 23 weeks, your baby's brain is developing at an astonishing rate, with new neural connections forming by the millions each day. The ears are getting better at picking up sounds, and your baby is developing a preference for familiar voices — that's you. The lungs are continuing to develop their branching airways, and the earliest precursors of surfactant — the substance that will eventually keep the tiny air sacs from sticking together — are beginning to develop. The skin is becoming less translucent as more layers form, though it's still quite thin.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom might notice that the baby's movements are becoming more predictable — active times and quiet times throughout the day. Braxton Hicks contractions might be more noticeable, especially after physical activity or dehydration. Her skin might feel itchy as it stretches across the growing belly — a good moisturizer or belly oil can help, but persistent itching (especially on hands and feet) should be mentioned to the doctor. Emotionally, being past the halfway point and feeling the baby move regularly brings a growing sense of connection.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Your baby's brain is forming millions of neural connections, and healthy fats and protein support this rapid development. A quick brain-building breakfast: mash avocado with lemon, salt, and pepper on whole grain toast, top with eggs (poached, fried, or scrambled), cherry tomatoes, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds and za'atar. Avocado provides monounsaturated fats, eggs add protein and choline, and the whole grain bread contributes fiber and B vitamins.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
The second trimester is often when exercise feels best. A structured walking routine helps maintain fitness — aim for 20-30 minutes at a conversational pace, four to five times per week. Include gentle inclines if comfortable, stay hydrated, and wear supportive shoes. Walking reduces the risk of gestational diabetes, helps manage weight gain, improves mood, and can even support better sleep.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
At 23 weeks, the pregnancy might be feeling more real with each passing day. The baby responds to sounds, the belly is clearly showing, and you're well past halfway. Some dads feel a wave of protectiveness at this stage; others feel anxious about all the preparations still to do. Both reactions are normal. Keep showing up, keep asking questions, keep being present.

## 👫 Couple Activity
Since your baby can now hear quite well, spend some time tonight exploring sounds together. Play different types of music — classical, jazz, your favorites — and watch for the baby's reaction. Try reading a short story aloud, or just talk to the belly. Research shows babies remember sounds they hear repeatedly in the womb, so this is the start of a beautiful connection.
