# Week 23, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's hearing is getting sharper. By now, they can hear loud sounds from outside the womb and may startle in response — a dog barking, a door slamming, or a sudden burst of music might trigger a visible jump that your partner can feel. The inner ear structures responsible for hearing are maturing, and your baby is particularly tuned into rhythmic, low-frequency sounds. Inside the womb, the soundtrack is constant: your partner's heartbeat, blood flowing through vessels, the rumble of her digestive system. It's actually quite loud in there — about the volume of a vacuum cleaner.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 23 weeks, your partner might be experiencing some new pains in unexpected places. Rib pain can start as the uterus expands upward. Hip pain at night is common, especially on the side she sleeps on. Sciatica — a sharp pain that radiates from the lower back down through the buttock and leg — affects some pregnant women as the growing uterus puts pressure on the sciatic nerve. These are all normal but can be uncomfortable. A supportive mattress, strategic pillow placement, and gentle stretching can help.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Fiber focus:** Constipation is one of the less glamorous pregnancy symptoms, caused by hormones slowing digestion and the growing uterus pressing on the intestines. High-fiber foods are the best natural remedy. Today's tip: overnight oats. Before bed, mix 1/2 cup oats, 1/2 cup milk or yogurt, 1 tbsp chia seeds, and a handful of berries in a jar. Refrigerate overnight. Morning breakfast is ready with no cooking — and loaded with fiber, protein, and antioxidants.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Sciatica relief stretches:**

**Pigeon Pose (Modified):**
1. Start on all fours
2. Bring right knee forward, placing it behind right wrist
3. Extend left leg straight back
4. Lower onto forearms or a pillow for support
5. Hold 30 seconds, breathing deeply
6. Switch sides

**Seated Spinal Twist:**
1. Sit cross-legged (or in a chair)
2. Place right hand on left knee, left hand behind you
3. Gently twist to the left, looking over left shoulder
4. Hold 20 seconds, switch sides

These can provide significant relief from sciatic nerve pressure.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
If your partner is dealing with rib or hip pain at night, here are some practical things you can do: help her arrange pillows before bed (between knees, under belly, behind back), offer to rub her lower back for a few minutes, and get up to bring her water so she doesn't have to roll out of bed herself. These small nighttime assists make a big difference when sleep is already challenging.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
The inside of the womb is not silent — it's about 80-90 decibels, roughly as loud as a lawnmower or busy restaurant. That's why "white noise" machines work so well for newborns: they recreate the constant ambient sound the baby is used to. Some parents record the sound of a washing machine or hair dryer — both surprisingly effective baby soothers because they mimic the womb's acoustic environment.

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
If your partner keeps waking up with hip pain on the side she's sleeping on, a memory foam mattress topper can make a significant difference. The softer surface distributes weight more evenly, reducing pressure points. Another trick: a thin pillow or folded towel under the waist (between the ribs and hips) when side-sleeping can prevent the lower spine from sagging and pulling on the hips. The investment in sleep comfort now pays dividends in mood, energy, and overall health for both of you.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
Start thinking about your workload for the final trimester and the weeks around the birth. Are there projects you should wrap up earlier? Colleagues who need to be trained on your responsibilities? Clients who should know you'll be out? Having these conversations early — with your own boss and team — shows professionalism and makes your paternity leave smoother. It also sets a positive example: taking paternity leave seriously signals that involved fatherhood is a priority, not an afterthought.
