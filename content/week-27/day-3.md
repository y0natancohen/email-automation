# Week 27, Day 4

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is busy practicing three essential skills: sucking, swallowing, and breathing. Coordinating all three is actually complex, and mastery is needed before feeding after birth. Right now, your little one is sucking on fingers and toes (yes, those toes are reachable!), swallowing amniotic fluid, and making breathing movements with developing lungs — a full rehearsal for life outside the womb.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's taste buds are fully formed, and they can actually taste what the Mom eats through the amniotic fluid. Studies show that babies whose mothers ate certain foods during pregnancy showed a preference for those flavors after birth. The Mom is already shaping your baby's palate.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom's center of gravity has shifted significantly, which can make her feel a bit clumsy or off-balance — totally normal as the body adjusts to new weight distribution. Her belly button might be starting to pop outward (it'll go back after birth). Increased vaginal discharge is also common and usually nothing to worry about unless it changes color or smell.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
A quick DHA-rich salmon bowl is perfect for supporting brain development: bake a salmon fillet with lemon and dill at 200 C for 12-15 minutes, serve over brown rice or quinoa with steamed broccoli, sliced cucumber, edamame, a drizzle of soy sauce and sesame oil, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Quick, nourishing, and leftovers make a great next-day lunch.

## 🌍 The Womb Soundscape
The womb is far from quiet. Your baby hears the constant whooshing of blood flow, the gurgling of digestion, and the Mom's heartbeat at about 80-90 decibels — roughly the volume of a vacuum cleaner. This is why white noise machines work so well for newborns. External sounds, especially voices and music, also filter through, and lower-pitched sounds like a dad's voice travel more easily through tissue and fluid.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Walking remains one of the best third-trimester exercises. If longer walks are becoming uncomfortable, try three 10-minute walks instead of one 30-minute walk — just as beneficial. If the weather isn't cooperating, walking around a mall works too. Consistent, gentle movement is the goal.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
This is a great week to start researching pediatricians. Many popular ones fill up quickly. Ask friends for recommendations, check with your insurance, and consider a meet-and-greet appointment. Things to weigh: location, office hours, after-hours availability, and whether their communication style feels right for your family.
