# Week 27, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby can now open and close their eyes! This is a huge milestone. While there's not much to see in there, the retinas are developing layers that will eventually detect light. The eyes are also developing the ability to focus. Your baby blinks, and the eyelids now have tiny eyelashes. The iris doesn't have its final color yet — that won't be determined until weeks or even months after birth (so no guessing eye color just yet!).

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Sleep may be getting trickier. Between the growing belly, frequent bathroom trips, leg cramps, and an active baby who loves to party at 2 AM, your partner might be feeling more fatigued. Restless legs syndrome (an uncomfortable urge to move the legs) can also appear around this time. It's annoying but usually harmless. Stretching before bed and staying hydrated can help.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Magnesium can help with leg cramps and restless legs, both common in the third trimester. Great sources include dark chocolate (yes, really!), almonds, bananas, and avocados. An evening snack of a banana with a small handful of almonds can help ease nighttime leg cramps. Also — staying well-hydrated throughout the day makes a big difference.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Gentle stretching before bed can significantly improve sleep quality. Try this 5-minute routine with your partner:
- Calf stretches against a wall (30 seconds each leg)
- Seated forward fold (modified with legs apart for belly room)
- Cat-cow stretches on hands and knees
- Side-lying hip stretch

Doing this together makes it more likely to become a habit — and it's a nice way to wind down the day together.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start paying attention to your partner's sleep comfort. Is the pillow situation working? Many women swear by a pregnancy pillow (the big U-shaped or C-shaped ones) in the third trimester. If she doesn't have one, it could be a thoughtful gift. Also, make sure the bedroom is cool — pregnant women tend to run warmer, and a cooler room helps with sleep quality.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one thing about our daily routine that you think will change the most after the baby comes? What's one thing you hope stays the same?"

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies at 27 weeks have a surprisingly strong grip. If you could reach in and offer your finger, your baby would grab onto it. This grasp reflex is one of the earliest reflexes to develop and will be one of the first things you notice after birth — that incredible moment when tiny fingers wrap around yours.

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
The left side is often recommended as the ideal sleeping position during pregnancy because it improves blood flow to the baby and helps the kidneys work more efficiently. But here's the truth: any side is fine. If your partner wakes up on her back, there's no need to panic — the body usually signals discomfort before any issues arise. The goal is simply to avoid prolonged back sleeping. A pillow between the knees and one supporting the belly can make side sleeping much more comfortable.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If your partner is still working, now is a good time for her to start thinking about her maternity leave plan. Who will cover her responsibilities? Are there projects she wants to wrap up before leave? Having a conversation with her manager about the transition plan reduces stress significantly. And for you — have you thought about your paternity leave? Check your company's policy and start planning how you'll use your time.
