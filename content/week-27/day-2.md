# Week 27, Day 2

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's brain activity is ramping up significantly. Neural connections are forming at an astonishing rate, and higher brain functions are coming online. Your little one now has distinct sleep and wake cycles, and during REM sleep, the brain is firing away — some researchers believe babies may even be dreaming at this stage. What do they dream about? Probably the sounds, sensations, and rhythms of their cozy world.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might notice that her belly feels tight from time to time — those are likely Braxton Hicks contractions. She may also be dealing with heartburn more frequently as the growing uterus pushes up against the stomach. Small, frequent meals can help. Some women also notice increased shortness of breath, which is totally normal as the body works harder to supply oxygen to both mom and baby.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids are crucial for your baby's brain development, and this is peak brain-building time. Great sources include salmon, sardines, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds. If your partner isn't a fan of fish, a prenatal DHA supplement (talk to the doctor first) can bridge the gap. Even adding a tablespoon of ground flaxseed to morning oatmeal or a smoothie makes a difference.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Third Trimester Exercise Focus:** Pelvic floor exercises are your partner's best friend right now. Kegels help strengthen the muscles that support the uterus, bladder, and bowels — crucial for birth and recovery. Here's a simple routine:
- Squeeze the pelvic floor muscles (like stopping the flow of urine) for 5 seconds
- Release for 5 seconds
- Repeat 10 times
- Do this 3 times a day

Your partner can do these anywhere — sitting at her desk, watching TV, or lying in bed. Nobody will even know!

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start thinking about your birth support toolkit. This isn't about buying things — it's about learning what helps during labor. Things like counter-pressure on the lower back, knowing how to help with breathing techniques, having a playlist ready, or simply knowing when to offer water and when to just be quiet and present. You'll learn more in childbirth class, but starting to think about it now gives you time.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby hiccups in the womb, and your partner can actually feel them! They happen because the diaphragm is practicing its movements. They feel like tiny, rhythmic taps — very different from kicks. Some babies hiccup multiple times a day. It's totally normal and actually a sign of healthy development.

## 🤔 Weekly Q&A — Answer
**Yesterday's question:** What are Braxton Hicks contractions, and how do you tell them from real labor?

**Answer:** Braxton Hicks are "practice" contractions that help tone the uterine muscle. They're usually irregular, don't get closer together, and often stop if your partner changes position, walks around, or drinks water. Real labor contractions are regular, get progressively closer together and stronger, and don't stop with movement. A helpful rule: if she can talk through it and it goes away with rest, it's likely Braxton Hicks. If contractions are regular and increasing in intensity, that's worth a call to the doctor.
