# Week 28, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's eyesight is developing rapidly. While it's dim inside the womb, your baby can now detect light filtering through the abdominal wall. If your partner is outside in bright sunlight, a warm glow reaches the baby. The eyes are practicing focusing, though true visual acuity won't develop until well after birth. Right now, the world is soft shapes and varying shades of light — a gentle introduction to seeing.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might be noticing more vivid and sometimes bizarre dreams. Pregnancy hormones, combined with lighter sleep (thanks to frequent waking), mean she's more likely to remember dreams. Some women dream about the baby, about labor, or about completely random things (showing up to a job interview as a penguin, for example). These dreams are normal and often reflect the brain processing all the changes and anticipation of this period.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Fiber is your partner's friend right now. Constipation is a common third-trimester complaint, and fiber helps keep things moving. Great high-fiber snacks: an apple with the skin on, a handful of almonds, popcorn (air-popped), or a small bowl of oatmeal. Pair fiber with plenty of water — fiber without hydration can actually make things worse. Aim for 25-30 grams of fiber per day.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Breathing exercises aren't just for labor — they're useful right now too. As the uterus grows and pushes up against the diaphragm, your partner may feel short of breath. Practice diaphragmatic breathing together:
- Sit or lie comfortably
- Place one hand on the chest, one on the belly
- Breathe in through the nose, feeling the belly rise (not the chest)
- Exhale slowly through the mouth
- Repeat for 2-3 minutes

This technique also helps reduce stress and anxiety.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Have you talked about the hospital route yet? It sounds basic, but knowing exactly how to get to the hospital (or birthing center) — and having a backup route in case of traffic — reduces stress enormously when the time comes. Do a practice drive at different times of day. Know where to park. Know which entrance to use. These small preparations make a big difference when adrenaline is running high.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one fear about the birth that we haven't said out loud yet? Let's name it so it has less power over us."

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies at 28 weeks can survive outside the womb with specialized medical care, and survival rates at this gestational age are above 90% in modern medical facilities. While every day in the womb helps, it's reassuring to know that medicine has made incredible advances in neonatal care. Your baby is already a viable little person!

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
If your partner is struggling to get comfortable at night, try the "pillow fortress" approach: one pillow between the knees, one under the belly, one behind the back, and one to hug. It sounds excessive, but the support makes a huge difference. If she doesn't have a pregnancy pillow, a regular body pillow or even rolled-up towels can work. The key is supporting the belly so it doesn't pull on the back, and keeping the hips aligned with a pillow between the knees.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If you haven't already, this is a good time to understand your rights regarding parental leave. In Israel, maternity leave (Hufsha la-Leda) provides specific protections and benefits. Make sure both of you understand: how much leave is available, what's paid vs. unpaid, how to apply through Bituach Leumi (National Insurance), and what your respective employers' policies are beyond the legal minimum. Knowledge is power — and reduces last-minute stress.
