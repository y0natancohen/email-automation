# Week 28, Day 7

## 👶 Baby Development
As week 28 wraps up, your baby is thriving. The eyes can track light, the ears can process sound, and the brain is building new connections every second. Your baby weighs about 2.2 pounds and is increasingly plump. The digestive system is practicing too — your baby swallows amniotic fluid, which passes through the stomach and intestines, preparing the GI tract for its future job.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner has now completed two-thirds of her pregnancy — that's worth celebrating! She might feel a sense of "can we fast-forward to the end?" mixed with "wait, I'm not ready yet." Both feelings are valid. Physically, she may notice that bending over is becoming more of an event, and putting on shoes might require some creative maneuvering. The belly is starting to command its own personal space.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Hydration is often overlooked but critically important. Your partner should be drinking about 10-12 cups of water daily. Staying hydrated helps prevent urinary tract infections (more common in pregnancy), reduces swelling, prevents constipation, and can help minimize Braxton Hicks contractions. If plain water gets boring, add slices of cucumber, lemon, or fresh mint. Herbal teas (like rooibos or chamomile) also count.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Saturday strolls are a great tradition. If you can, walk somewhere beautiful — a park, a waterfront, or just a quiet neighborhood. Keep the pace comfortable and bring water. If your partner needs breaks, take them without comment. Sometimes the best exercise is the kind you don't notice because you're enjoying the conversation and the scenery.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start thinking about your work schedule around the due date. Talk to your employer about your plans: when you intend to start leave, how much notice you'll give, and whether you can work flexibly in the weeks leading up to the due date. Having this conversation early gives your employer time to plan and gives you peace of mind. The last thing you want is to feel stressed about work when you should be focused on your partner and baby.

## 💬 Conversation Starter

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby already has the same number of neurons they'll have as an adult — roughly 100 billion! The difference is in the connections between them. After birth, the brain will spend years wiring and rewiring these connections based on experiences, interactions, and learning. Every time you talk to, play with, or comfort your baby, you'll literally be building their brain architecture.

## 🔮 After-Birth Preview: Cord Blood Banking
You may have heard about cord blood banking — the practice of saving blood from the umbilical cord after birth. Cord blood contains stem cells that can be used to treat certain diseases. You have options: donate it to a public bank (free, available to anyone who needs it), store it privately (a paid service, reserved for your family), or let it be disposed of. There's no pressure to decide right now, but it's worth researching and discussing before the birth. Talk to your doctor about what makes sense for your family.

## 🙏 Rest & Gratitude
You're 28 weeks in. In football terms, you're starting the fourth quarter. Take a moment to acknowledge everything your partner's body has done — and everything it will continue to do. And take a moment for yourself too. You've been showing up, learning, supporting, and preparing. You're going to be a great dad. Not because you're perfect, but because you care enough to be reading this right now.
