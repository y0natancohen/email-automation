# Week 28, Day 6

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's lungs continue to mature, producing more surfactant with each passing day. The blood vessels in the lungs are also developing, preparing for that first breath of air. Meanwhile, your baby is getting plumper — fat is accumulating under the skin, smoothing out those wrinkles and giving your baby that adorable newborn roundness. The skin is transitioning from translucent to more opaque as the fat layers build up.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
By Friday, your partner might be feeling the cumulative weight of the week. Third-trimester fatigue is different from first-trimester exhaustion — it's more about physical discomfort than hormonal drowsiness. She might feel like she can't get comfortable in any position. Encourage her to rest when she can, without guilt. Growing a human is a full-time job on top of whatever else she's doing.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Shabbat Comfort Food: Sweet Potato and Chickpea Stew**
- 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 can chickpeas, drained
- 1 can coconut milk
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon curry powder, 1 teaspoon cumin
- Handful of spinach
- Salt and pepper to taste

Saut onion and garlic, add spices, then sweet potatoes and chickpeas. Pour in coconut milk, simmer 20-25 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender. Stir in spinach at the end. Serve over rice or with crusty bread. Warm, nourishing, and full of iron, fiber, and vitamin A.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Today, focus on relaxation rather than exertion. A warm (not hot) bath can work wonders for aching muscles and swollen feet. Add some Epsom salts for extra relief — the magnesium absorbs through the skin and can help with muscle cramps. Keep the water temperature comfortable, not steaming hot, and limit bath time to about 20 minutes.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
As Shabbat begins, take over the evening prep. Light candles together (or ask your partner to rest while you handle dinner). The act of creating a peaceful, welcoming space at the end of the week is a form of care for your growing family. It's also good practice — after the baby arrives, you'll be taking on more household responsibilities, and building those habits now makes the transition smoother.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can now blink! The eyelids have been fused shut for most of the pregnancy, but now they're fully functional. Your baby blinks, squints, and even furrows their brow. These facial expressions aren't intentional yet — they're reflexes — but they're practicing the same expressions they'll use to charm you after birth.

## 💰 Financial Tip
Start looking into baby health insurance now. In Israel, all children are covered under the national health insurance system, but you'll need to register your baby with a Kupat Cholim (health fund) after birth. Research the different funds — Clalit, Maccabi, Meuhedet, and Leumit — and compare their supplementary services for infants (like additional vaccinations, developmental checkups, and pediatric specialists). Some offer welcome packages for newborns too. Making this decision now means one less thing to figure out in those hazy first weeks.

## 🧘 Mindfulness Moment
Tonight, try a "body scan" together. Lie down comfortably (your partner on her side, you however you like). Starting from the toes, slowly bring attention to each part of the body, noticing sensations without judgment. Move up through the legs, belly, chest, arms, neck, and face. It takes about 10 minutes and is incredibly calming. For your partner, it can help her reconnect with her body in a positive way — sometimes in the third trimester, the body feels like it's been taken over, and this exercise restores a sense of ownership and appreciation.

## 🕯️ Shabbat Activity
**Gratitude Walk:** Before or after dinner, take a slow walk together (even just around the block). As you walk, take turns naming things you're grateful for — big or small. The sunset, each other, the baby's hiccups, a good friend, a comfortable bed. This simple practice shifts the focus from anxiety about the future to appreciation for the present. And the walking helps with your partner's circulation too.
