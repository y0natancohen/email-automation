# Week 28, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Your baby is about 14.8 inches long and weighs around 2.2 pounds this week. Exciting development: tiny eyelashes are growing! Your baby's eyesight is developing, and they can now sense light filtering through the womb. The brain is building billions of neurons, and REM sleep is increasing — meaning more dreaming. Antibodies are transferring from your partner to the baby, building up that little immune system. Some babies begin positioning head-down this week, though there's still plenty of room to move around. If your partner is Rh-negative, this is typically when the Rh factor shot (RhoGAM) is given.

## 👶 Baby Development
Those delicate eyelashes are now in place, framing eyes that can open and close and even sense changes in light. Shine a flashlight on your partner's belly and your baby might respond by turning toward or away from it. The brain is in overdrive — building billions of neurons and forming the neural networks that will eventually allow your baby to think, feel, and learn. REM sleep is increasing, and your baby is spending more time in dream states.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner may notice that her belly has "popped" even more this week — the growth can feel rapid in the third trimester. She might experience more Braxton Hicks contractions, especially after physical activity or if she's dehydrated. Varicose veins may become more noticeable in the legs, and round ligament pain (sharp twinges on the sides of the belly) can happen with sudden movements. Remind her to move slowly when changing positions.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Vitamin C is essential for iron absorption and for your partner's immune system. A simple snack: red bell pepper strips with hummus. Red bell peppers have almost three times more vitamin C than oranges! Pair this with iron-rich foods at meals to maximize absorption. Other great vitamin C sources: strawberries, kiwi, broccoli, and citrus fruits.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
If your partner enjoys swimming, the third trimester is the best time to take advantage of it. The buoyancy of water relieves pressure on joints and the spine, reduces swelling, and provides a full-body workout without the impact. Even just floating or gentle laps can feel incredible. Many community pools offer aqua-natal classes specifically designed for pregnant women.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Week 28 is a common time for the glucose tolerance test (checking for gestational diabetes). If your partner has this test this week, know that she'll need to drink a very sweet glucose drink and then wait an hour for blood work. Offer to go with her — the waiting room can feel long and the drink can cause nausea. Bring a book, some snacks (for after the test), and keep her company.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's something about pregnancy that has surprised you the most — something you didn't expect?"

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby is now dreaming! REM (rapid eye movement) sleep is well established by week 28. While we can't know exactly what babies dream about, researchers believe they're processing the sensory experiences of their world: sounds, movements, and the feeling of being held in warm amniotic fluid. It's like a cozy preview of consciousness.

## 👫 Couple Activity
**Letter to Baby:** Each of you sit down separately and write a short letter to your baby. It doesn't have to be long — just a few sentences about what you're feeling right now, what you hope for them, or what you want them to know about this time in your lives. Seal them in envelopes, date them, and save them to give to your child someday. This is one of those things that costs nothing but becomes priceless.
