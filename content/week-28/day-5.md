# Week 28, Day 5

## 👶 Baby Development
Some babies begin to shift into a head-down position around this time, though many won't settle into their final birth position for several more weeks. Your baby still has room to somersault, flip, and rotate, so don't worry if they're currently breech (head-up) or transverse (sideways). There's plenty of time. Your baby is also putting on weight steadily now — building the fat layers that will help maintain body temperature after birth.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
If your partner is Rh-negative and the baby is (or might be) Rh-positive, she may receive a RhoGAM injection around this time. This prevents her body from producing antibodies against the baby's blood, which could cause complications in future pregnancies. It's a quick injection and one of those routine but important steps. Beyond that, your partner might notice increased back pain as the belly grows. A maternity support belt can help distribute the weight more evenly.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Protein needs are higher in the third trimester — about 75-100 grams per day. But it doesn't have to come from meat. Great protein sources: Greek yogurt (15g per cup), lentils (18g per cup cooked), eggs (6g each), chickpeas (15g per cup), and cottage cheese (14g per half cup). A chickpea salad for lunch with an egg on top is an easy way to hit 20+ grams in one meal.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
The pelvic tilt is a great exercise for back pain relief. Here's how:
- Get on hands and knees (tabletop position)
- Inhale, let the belly drop and arch the back slightly
- Exhale, tuck the pelvis under and round the back (like a cat)
- Repeat 10 times, slowly

This gently mobilizes the spine and strengthens the core muscles that support the back. It can also help encourage the baby into an optimal position.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Have a candid conversation with your partner about the birth plan this week. Not a formal document yet — just a conversation. Topics to explore: How does she feel about pain medication? Does she want to try natural pain management first? Are there specific positions she wants to try during labor? Who does she want in the room? What matters most to her about the experience? Listen more than you talk. This is about understanding her vision so you can support it.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What kind of dad do you think I'll be? What kind of mom do you think you'll be? Let's be honest and encouraging."

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's adrenal glands are now producing hormones — including cortisol, which helps the lungs mature. It's one of those brilliant design features: the baby's own body is preparing itself for the transition to breathing air. The closer to full term, the more surfactant the lungs produce, making each day in the womb count toward easier breathing after birth.

## 🤔 Weekly Q&A — Answer
**Wednesday's question:** Is it normal for the baby to have hiccups frequently?

**Answer:** Absolutely normal! Fetal hiccups are caused by the baby's diaphragm contracting as it practices breathing movements. They can happen several times a day and are actually a sign of healthy development. Your partner will feel them as rhythmic, repetitive tapping — very different from kicks or rolls. They usually last a few minutes and then stop on their own. Some babies hiccup more than others, just like adults. The only time to mention it to a doctor is if hiccups seem to increase dramatically in the last few weeks of pregnancy, though even this is usually nothing to worry about.

## 🧠 Mental Health Check-In
Let's talk about "imposter syndrome" as a parent-to-be. That voice that says "Am I really ready for this?" or "What if I'm bad at this?" — almost every first-time parent hears it. Here's the truth: the fact that you're worried about being a good parent is itself evidence that you'll be one. People who don't care about doing well don't spend time worrying about it. You're going to make mistakes — every parent does — and you'll learn from them. Perfection isn't the goal. Love, presence, and effort are.

## 💕 Intimacy Corner
The second trimester might have been a time of increased desire for some couples, but the third trimester often brings a shift. Your partner might feel too uncomfortable, too tired, or simply not in the mood — and that's completely okay. Don't take it personally. What matters now is maintaining emotional closeness. Try giving her a foot massage tonight with some lotion. It's nurturing, it helps with swelling, and it communicates love without any pressure. Physical touch can be deeply intimate without being sexual.
