# Week 28, Day 2

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's brain is producing billions of neurons at a breathtaking pace. These neurons are forming connections — synapses — that will be the foundation for everything from breathing to laughing to learning a language. The brain's surface is starting to develop more folds and grooves, increasing the surface area where all this complex processing happens. Your baby is literally getting smarter every day.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Heartburn might be ramping up as the growing uterus pushes the stomach upward. Your partner might also notice more pronounced swelling in her hands and feet, especially by the end of the day. If swelling is sudden or severe, especially in the face, it's worth a call to the doctor (this can be a sign of preeclampsia). Normal swelling, though, is just an annoying part of the third trimester and usually eases with rest and elevation.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
To combat heartburn: smaller, more frequent meals work better than three large ones. Avoiding spicy, fatty, and acidic foods before bed can help. Encourage your partner to eat her last meal at least two hours before lying down. Ginger tea (not ginger ale — that's mostly sugar) can also soothe the stomach. And elevating the head of the bed slightly with an extra pillow can reduce nighttime reflux.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Third Trimester Exercise Focus: Upper Body Strength**
Carrying a baby requires arm and back strength! Here's a simple routine:
- Wall push-ups: 10 reps, 3 sets
- Seated bicep curls with light weights (2-5 lbs): 10 reps each arm
- Shoulder blade squeezes: squeeze shoulder blades together, hold 5 seconds, release. Repeat 10 times.
- Seated cat-cow for the spine: arch and round the back gently while sitting on a stability ball or chair

These exercises also help with posture, which takes a hit during pregnancy.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start thinking about your support role during labor. Many dads wonder, "What will I actually DO?" The answer: more than you think. You'll be her advocate (making sure her wishes are communicated to the medical team), her comfort provider (cold cloths, back rubs, words of encouragement), and her anchor (a calm, steady presence when things feel intense). You don't need to have all the answers — you just need to be there, fully present.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
By week 28, your baby can distinguish between sweet and bitter tastes in the amniotic fluid. When the fluid is sweeter (after your partner eats something sweet), babies swallow more. When it's bitter, they swallow less. Your little one already has preferences!

## 🤔 Weekly Q&A — Answer
**Yesterday's question:** What is kick counting, and when should we start?

**Answer:** Kick counting (also called fetal movement counting) is a simple way to monitor your baby's well-being. Starting around week 28, your partner can begin tracking how long it takes to feel 10 movements (kicks, rolls, jabs — anything counts). Pick a time when the baby is usually active, lie on the left side, and count. Most babies will reach 10 movements within 30 minutes to 2 hours. The goal isn't to hit a specific number, but to learn your baby's normal pattern. If movements feel significantly decreased, contact your healthcare provider. It's a simple tool that gives peace of mind.
