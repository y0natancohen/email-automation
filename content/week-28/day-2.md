# Week 28, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's eyesight is developing rapidly. While it's dim inside the womb, your baby can now detect light filtering through the abdominal wall. If the Mom is outside in bright sunlight, a warm glow reaches the baby. The eyes are practicing focusing, though true visual acuity won't develop until well after birth. Right now, the world is soft shapes and varying shades of light — a gentle introduction to seeing.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies at 28 weeks can survive outside the womb with specialized medical care, and survival rates at this gestational age are above 90% in modern facilities. While every day in the womb helps, it's reassuring to know that medicine has made incredible advances in neonatal care.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom might be having more vivid and sometimes bizarre dreams. Pregnancy hormones combined with lighter sleep mean she's more likely to remember them. These dreams are normal and often reflect the brain processing all the changes and anticipation of this period.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Fiber is the Mom's friend right now. Constipation is a common third-trimester complaint, and fiber helps keep things moving. Great high-fiber snacks: an apple with the skin on, a handful of almonds, air-popped popcorn, or a small bowl of oatmeal. Pair fiber with plenty of water — fiber without hydration can actually make things worse. Aim for 25-30 grams of fiber per day.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Diaphragmatic breathing is useful both now and during labor. As the uterus pushes against the diaphragm, the Mom may feel short of breath. Practice together: sit comfortably, place one hand on the chest and one on the belly, breathe in through the nose feeling the belly rise (not the chest), and exhale slowly through the mouth. Repeat for 2-3 minutes. This also helps reduce stress and anxiety.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Have you talked about the hospital route yet? Knowing exactly how to get there — and having a backup route — reduces stress enormously when the time comes. Do a practice drive at different times of day. Know where to park and which entrance to use. These small preparations make a big difference when adrenaline is running high.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one fear about the birth that we haven't said out loud yet? Let's name it so it has less power over us."

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
If the Mom is struggling to get comfortable at night, try the "pillow fortress" approach: one between the knees, one under the belly, one behind the back, and one to hug. The support makes a huge difference — keeping the belly supported so it doesn't pull on the back, and the hips aligned with a pillow between the knees.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If you haven't already, this is a good time to understand your rights regarding parental leave. In Israel, maternity leave provides specific protections and benefits. Make sure both of you understand how much leave is available, what's paid vs. unpaid, how to apply through Bituach Leumi, and what your employers' policies are beyond the legal minimum.
