# Week 24, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to Week 24! Your baby is now about 11.8 inches long and weighs approximately 1.3 pounds — roughly the size of an ear of corn. This week brings a landmark sensory development: the inner ear is fully developed, giving your baby a true sense of balance. They know when your partner is standing, sitting, lying down, or bending over. They can sense motion and orientation in space. The face is now fully formed with all features in place, and taste buds are complete. The brain is growing rapidly, with visible folds and grooves developing on its surface. This is also the beginning of the glucose screening window (24-28 weeks), which tests for gestational diabetes. We'll talk about what to expect and how to prepare. It's a big week of growth, sensing, and milestones.

## 👶 Baby Development
The inner ear reaching full development is a game-changer. The vestibular system — responsible for balance and spatial orientation — is now functional. Your baby can sense gravity and movement, which is why some pregnant women notice the baby moving more when they change positions or lie down after being upright. The tiny semicircular canals in the inner ear are filled with fluid that shifts with movement, sending signals to the brain about position and motion. This is the same system that gives us the ability to stand upright and maintain balance. Your baby is essentially doing vestibular training, getting ready for a world where up and down matter.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 24 weeks, your partner's uterus is now about the size of a soccer ball and sits at or just above the belly button. She might be experiencing back pain as the additional weight shifts her posture, and the hormone relaxin continues to loosen her joints. Some women develop linea nigra — a dark line running from the belly button down — which is a harmless melanin change that fades after birth. Appetite might be strong, and she may notice she's gaining about a pound per week (this is normal and expected). Her energy levels are still generally good, though they may start declining as the third trimester approaches.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Glucose-Friendly Mediterranean Bowl (Full Recipe)**

With the glucose test approaching, now's a great time to model balanced blood sugar eating.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 cup cooked quinoa
- Grilled chicken breast, sliced
- 1/2 cup roasted sweet potato
- Handful of arugula
- 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 2 tbsp tahini
- Squeeze of lemon
- Pomegranate seeds for topping

*Directions:*
1. Layer quinoa in a bowl
2. Arrange chicken, sweet potato, arugula, and tomatoes on top
3. Drizzle with tahini thinned with lemon juice and a splash of water
4. Scatter pomegranate seeds on top

This bowl combines protein, complex carbs, healthy fats, and fiber — the perfect formula for stable blood sugar. The quinoa and sweet potato provide slow-release energy without causing sugar spikes.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Balance-focused exercises** are particularly relevant this week, since your baby is developing their own sense of balance:

**Single Leg Stand:**
1. Stand near a wall or chair for support
2. Lift one foot slightly off the ground
3. Hold for 10-15 seconds
4. Switch legs
5. Repeat 5 times each side

**Heel-Toe Walking:**
1. Walk in a straight line, placing the heel of one foot directly in front of the toes of the other
2. Walk 10 steps forward, then 10 steps back
3. Use arms out to the sides for balance if needed

As your partner's center of gravity shifts, practicing balance exercises helps prevent falls and builds confidence in movement.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
The vestibular system (inner ear balance) is one of the first sensory systems to fully develop, and it's critical for brain development. The rocking motion a baby experiences in the womb — as the mother walks, moves, and even breathes — stimulates this system and helps wire the brain for motor coordination. This is also why rocking a baby after birth is so soothing: it recreates the constant gentle motion they experienced in the womb.
