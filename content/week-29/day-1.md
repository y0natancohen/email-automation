# Week 29, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Your baby is now about 15.2 inches long and weighs around 2.5 pounds — roughly the size of a butternut squash. The big news this week: muscles and lungs are maturing rapidly, and your baby's bones are fully developed but still soft and flexible (which will be important for birth). As space gets tighter in there, movements will start to feel different — less like martial arts and more like slow stretches and rolls. Your baby can now taste the difference between sweet and bitter through the amniotic fluid, and the brain is actively controlling body temperature. For your partner, the increasing belly size means more adjustments to daily life.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's muscles are getting stronger every day, and the lungs are continuing their crucial development. The bones are fully formed but remain soft — they're made of flexible cartilage that will gradually harden after birth. This flexibility is by design: it allows the baby's body (especially the head) to compress slightly during the journey through the birth canal. Your baby's brain has reached a new milestone — it's now sophisticated enough to control body temperature, which means less reliance on the amniotic fluid for warmth.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might notice that the baby's movements feel different now. Instead of sharp kicks and jabs, she might feel more rolling, squirming, and stretching sensations. This is because the baby is getting bigger and has less room to windmill those arms and legs. Some women find this change alarming, but it's completely normal. The movements should still be regular — just different in character. She may also notice more pelvic pressure and lower back discomfort as the baby grows.

**Baked Egg Shakshuka with Feta and Herbs**
Your baby's bones are fully formed but still soft this week, and calcium plus vitamin D are critical for helping them strengthen. Eggs are one of the few food sources of vitamin D, and the feta adds a calcium boost.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 1 onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 6 large eggs
- 1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon cumin
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)
- Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish
- Crusty bread or challah for dipping

*Directions:*
1. Preheat oven to 375°F/190°C.
2. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Saute onion and bell pepper for 5 minutes until softened.
3. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and chili flakes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Pour in crushed tomatoes, stir, and simmer for 10 minutes until thickened slightly.
5. Make 6 small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each one.
6. Scatter feta cheese around the eggs.
7. Transfer to the oven and bake 10-12 minutes until egg whites are set but yolks are still slightly runny.
8. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with bread for dipping.

Each egg provides about 6g of protein and one of the few natural food sources of vitamin D, while the feta delivers calcium — both essential for your baby's developing skeletal system.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
As the belly grows, balance becomes more important. Try this simple balance exercise:
- Stand near a wall or countertop (for support if needed)
- Lift one foot slightly off the ground
- Hold for 10-15 seconds
- Switch sides
- Repeat 5 times each side

This strengthens the stabilizer muscles in the ankles and core, reducing the risk of falls as the center of gravity shifts.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
The nursery — have you started? If not, no panic, but this is a good time to begin planning. You don't need a Pinterest-perfect room. You need a safe crib (new, with a firm mattress), a place to change diapers, and somewhere to sit during night feedings (a comfortable chair or glider is worth its weight in gold). Start with these basics and add personality over time. And if you don't have a separate room for the nursery, that's fine — many families keep the baby in their room for the first 6-12 months, and that's actually recommended for safe sleep.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can distinguish between sweet and bitter tastes right now! Researchers have found that when a sweet substance is injected into the amniotic fluid, babies increase their swallowing rate. When a bitter substance is introduced, they decrease it. Your little one is already a picky eater — in the most adorable way possible.
