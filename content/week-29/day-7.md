# Week 29, Day 7

## 👶 Baby Development
Week 29 is wrapping up, and your baby is getting stronger by the hour. Those muscles that have been practicing all week are noticeably more powerful — your partner might see her belly move and shift as the baby stretches and repositions. The brain is continuing to develop at an extraordinary pace, adding layers of complexity that will eventually allow your baby to think, reason, and imagine. For now, the brain is focused on mastering the basics: breathing, swallowing, temperature regulation, and sleep-wake cycles.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner is roughly three-quarters through her pregnancy. She might feel like time is both flying and crawling — eager to meet the baby, but also hoping for a few more weeks of preparation. Physically, she may notice more pronounced waddling (the relaxin hormone is loosening her pelvic joints to prepare for birth, which changes her gait). Some women feel self-conscious about this, but it's actually her body doing something incredibly smart and purposeful.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
A satisfying weekend breakfast: overnight oats. Mix the night before and it's ready to eat in the morning — perfect for those low-energy mornings.
- 1/2 cup rolled oats
- 1/2 cup milk (or plant milk)
- 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
- 1 tablespoon chia seeds
- 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
- Top in the morning with berries, sliced banana, or nuts

Make two jars — one for each of you. It's an easy, fiber-rich, protein-packed start to the day.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Saturday is for rest and gentle movement. A walk together is perfect, or if the weather isn't cooperating, some gentle stretching at home. Focus on what feels good rather than pushing for any goal. If your partner has been dealing with back pain, today is a great day to apply a warm compress to the lower back while resting.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's a good exercise for today: practice installing and removing the car seat. Seriously. Do it three times. It should feel natural and easy by the time the baby arrives. You don't want to be fumbling with straps and latches in the hospital parking lot while holding a tiny newborn for the first time. Also, learn how to adjust the harness for a newborn — most car seats come set for larger babies and need to be tightened down significantly.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"In our first week home with the baby, what kind of support would help us most? Meal deliveries? A parent visiting? A postpartum doula? Let's think about this realistically."

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby is now about the same size and weight as a small coconut. But unlike a coconut, your baby is squishy, warm, and has the cutest little facial expressions. Ultrasounds at this stage sometimes catch babies smiling, frowning, or sticking out their tongues. These are reflexes, not intentional expressions, but they're absolutely adorable.

## 🔮 After-Birth Preview: The First Night at Home
Here's some honest truth about the first night home: it will probably feel surreal. After the buzz of the hospital (or birthing center), coming home with a tiny human can feel both magical and terrifying. The house will feel different — quieter and more significant. The baby will probably sleep a lot (they're exhausted from being born!), but they'll also wake up for feeding every 2-3 hours. Your job that night: keep the basics covered. Make sure there's food in the house, the baby's sleeping area is ready, and you and your partner support each other through the feeds and diaper changes. It won't be perfect, and it doesn't need to be. You'll find your rhythm.

## 🙏 Rest & Gratitude
Take a moment today to rest in gratitude for your baby's strong bones, developing lungs, and active brain. Your partner's body has been building a human for 29 weeks — every nutrient, every heartbeat, every breath contributing to this tiny life. And you've been there through it all, supporting, learning, and preparing. Give yourselves credit. You're doing beautifully.
