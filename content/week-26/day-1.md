# Week 26, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to Week 26 — the final week of the second trimester! Your baby is now about 14 inches long and weighs approximately 1.7 pounds, roughly the size of a zucchini. And this week brings one of the most magical milestones of the entire pregnancy: your baby is opening their eyes for the first time. After months of development behind closed eyelids, your baby can now blink, look around (in the dim light of the womb), and begin processing visual information. Brain wave activity for both hearing and sight is now detectable — scientists can measure your baby thinking. The lungs are getting stronger, practicing breathing by inhaling and exhaling amniotic fluid. And the immune system is starting to develop, preparing for the germ-filled world outside. This week marks the transition from the comfortable second trimester to the intense final stretch. You're in the home stretch, and your baby is getting ready.

## 👶 Baby Development
Eyes open. It's hard to overstate how significant this is. Your baby's eyelids have been fused shut since about week 11, allowing the delicate structures of the eye to develop in safety. Now, at 26 weeks, the eyelids separate, and your baby can open and close their eyes. The retina is developing its light-sensitive layers, and the optic nerve is transmitting signals to the brain. While the womb is mostly dark, some light does penetrate — if your partner shines a flashlight at her belly, the baby might turn toward or away from the light. The iris still doesn't have its final color (that's determined by melanin, which fills in later), but the basic eye structure is complete.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 26 weeks, the second trimester is coming to an end, and your partner's body is showing it. The belly is prominently visible, and she might be getting comments from strangers (welcome and unwelcome). She might notice more frequent Braxton Hicks contractions, especially in the evenings. Back pain and pelvic pressure are common companions. She might feel the baby hiccup several times a day. Emotionally, the approaching third trimester can bring a surge of nesting energy — wanting to organize, prepare, and get everything ready. Channel this productively but gently.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Immune-Supporting Chicken & Vegetable Stir-Fry (Full Recipe)**

Your baby's immune system is developing — support it with vitamin-rich foods.

*Ingredients:*
- 2 chicken breasts, sliced thin
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup broccoli florets
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp honey
- Rice or noodles for serving

*Directions:*
1. Cook chicken in sesame oil until golden (5 min), remove
2. In same pan, stir-fry vegetables 3-4 minutes (keep them crisp)
3. Add garlic and ginger, cook 30 seconds
4. Return chicken, add soy sauce and honey, toss
5. Serve over rice or noodles

Broccoli and bell pepper deliver vitamin C, ginger and garlic have natural immune-supporting properties, and chicken provides protein and zinc.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Deep Squat with Support:**
1. Stand facing a sturdy counter or table
2. Feet wider than hip-width, toes turned out
3. Hold the counter and lower into a deep squat
4. Keep heels on the floor, back straight
5. Hold for 30 seconds, breathing deeply
6. Stand slowly. Repeat 5 times

The deep squat opens the pelvis, stretches the pelvic floor, and is one of the most beneficial positions for preparing for birth. Many birthing professionals recommend spending time in this position daily during the third trimester.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Your baby can see now — or at least detect light and dark. Tonight, try this: in a dark room, gently hold a flashlight against your partner's belly (not directly on the skin — through a thin cloth). Move it slowly. Your baby might respond by moving toward or away from the light. Don't be disappointed if nothing happens — the response varies. But if you see or feel movement in response, you just had your first visual interaction with your child. That's extraordinary.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
When your baby opens their eyes in the womb, what do they see? The womb isn't completely dark — when your partner is in bright sunlight or has a light shining on her belly, a warm reddish glow permeates the uterus. It's similar to what you see when you close your eyes and face a bright light. This dim illumination helps the eyes calibrate and the visual cortex begin to process input. Your baby is seeing the world's softest, warmest light show.
