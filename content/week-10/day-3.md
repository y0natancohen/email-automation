# Week 10, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
A quiet revolution is happening inside your partner right now: ossification. That's the process where soft cartilage gradually hardens into bone. Your baby's skeleton is starting to solidify. The arms and legs are lengthening, and those newly separated fingers and toes can now bend at the joints. The spine is clearly visible, and tiny ribs are beginning to form. Your baby's forehead is prominent and bulging — that's because the brain is growing at a staggering rate, producing roughly 250,000 new neurons every minute.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Frequent urination is likely in full swing. The growing uterus is pressing directly on the bladder, and increased blood flow means the kidneys are filtering more fluid than usual. Your partner might feel like she lives in the bathroom. Headaches can also increase during this period, often caused by hormonal changes, dehydration, or fatigue. Encourage her to stay hydrated and rest when she can. Round ligament pain — a sharp, brief pain on the sides of the lower abdomen — may start to appear as the uterus grows.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Calcium is calling.** Your baby's bones are forming, and that takes calcium — a lot of it. If your partner isn't getting enough from food, her body will pull calcium from her own bones. Aim for 1,000 mg of calcium daily.

Quick high-calcium snack ideas:
- A cup of yogurt (300 mg calcium)
- A glass of fortified orange juice (350 mg)
- A handful of almonds (75 mg)
- A bowl of fortified cereal with milk (400+ mg)
- Two tablespoons of tahini (130 mg) — a great Israeli staple!

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Yoga Pose: Child's Pose (Balasana)**
This is one of the most comforting poses during pregnancy. Kneel on the floor, sit back on the heels, and fold forward with arms extended. If the belly gets in the way, widen the knees. Hold for 30 seconds to a minute. This gently stretches the back, hips, and thighs, and is incredibly calming. It's also a good position for relieving mild nausea.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
If your partner is dealing with headaches, offer a gentle neck and shoulder massage tonight. Use your thumbs to make small circles at the base of her skull and along the tops of her shoulders. Five minutes can make a world of difference. Dim the lights, skip the screens — create a calm environment. Acetaminophen (Tylenol/Acamol) is generally considered safe during pregnancy, but ibuprofen (Advil) is not.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"If you could give our future child one piece of life advice, what would it be?" Keep it to one sentence each. You might be surprised what comes out.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can now swallow. It's practicing by gulping tiny amounts of amniotic fluid, which the kidneys then process and excrete back into the amniotic sac. Yes — your baby is essentially drinking its own recycled bathwater. This loop is actually important for developing the digestive system and maintaining the right amount of amniotic fluid.

## 😴 Sleep Tip
Your partner might be finding sleep harder, even though she's exhausted. A body pillow or a regular pillow between the knees can help with hip and back discomfort. Sleeping on the left side is often recommended because it improves blood flow to the uterus and kidneys, though either side is fine at this stage. If she's waking up at 3 AM, a small protein-rich snack before bed (like a handful of nuts or cheese and crackers) can help stabilize blood sugar overnight.

## 💼 Workplace Pregnancy Tip
If your partner hasn't told her employer yet, she doesn't have to. In Israel, there's no legal obligation to disclose pregnancy until a certain point, and she's protected from dismissal during pregnancy under the Employment of Women Law. That said, if she needs accommodations — like extra bathroom breaks, flexible hours, or less exposure to chemicals or heavy lifting — it may help to disclose sooner. The timing is her call entirely.
