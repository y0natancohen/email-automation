# Week 10, Day 2

## 👶 Baby Development
Today's spotlight: tooth buds. Tiny clusters of cells are forming beneath your baby's gum line — these will eventually become 20 baby teeth. Your baby won't actually cut teeth until around 6 months after birth, but the foundation is being laid right now. Meanwhile, the outer ears are continuing to take shape, migrating from the neck area toward their final position on the sides of the head. Your baby's head is still large compared to the body (about half the total length), but the body is starting to catch up.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Fatigue might feel relentless. Your partner's body is building an entirely new organ (the placenta) while growing a human — that takes enormous energy. She may feel winded doing everyday tasks. Breast tenderness often peaks around this time. Visible veins on the chest and breasts might appear as blood volume increases by up to 50% during pregnancy. If she's experiencing food aversions, they're often strongest right now but tend to ease in the coming weeks.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Quick tip today: If nausea is making big meals impossible, try the "six small meals" approach. Keep crackers, nuts, and dried fruit within arm's reach. Ginger tea or ginger chews can genuinely help — ginger has been shown in studies to reduce pregnancy nausea. A squeeze of lemon in water first thing in the morning can also settle the stomach. Cold foods tend to be easier to tolerate than hot ones (less smell).

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Stretching Routine (10 minutes):**
1. **Cat-Cow Stretch** — On all fours, alternate between arching and rounding the back. 10 reps. Great for back pain.
2. **Side Stretch** — Stand with feet hip-width apart, raise one arm overhead and lean to the opposite side. Hold 15 seconds each side.
3. **Hip Circles** — Stand and gently circle hips in both directions. 10 circles each way.
4. **Calf Raises** — Rise up on toes, hold for 2 seconds, lower. 15 reps. Helps with leg cramps.
5. **Seated Forward Fold** — Sit with legs extended, gently fold forward from the hips. Hold 20 seconds. Don't push into discomfort.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Stock up on ginger ale, crackers, and her current "safe foods" — the foods she can actually stomach. Don't take it personally if she can't eat the dinner you made. Her body is being hijacked by hormones, and food aversions are involuntary. Having her go-to snacks ready shows you're paying attention.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one tradition from your family that you'd love to carry on with our kid? And is there anything you'd do differently?" This opens up a beautiful conversation about values and parenting styles.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At this stage, your baby is moving almost constantly — stretching, kicking, wiggling, and even hiccupping. But at just over an inch long, these movements are far too small for your partner to feel. She won't notice kicks until around weeks 18-22. Right now, your little one is basically doing gymnastics in a swimming pool the size of a tennis ball.

## 💡 Yesterday's Q&A Answer
Yesterday we asked: when do fingerprints form? The answer is **around week 13!** In just a few weeks, the friction ridges on your baby's fingertips will begin to develop, creating a pattern that is entirely unique — no one in the history of humanity has ever had or ever will have the same fingerprints as your child. Not even identical twins share fingerprints.

## 🏋️ Exercise Focus
**Why pelvic floor exercises matter now:** The pelvic floor is a hammock of muscles that supports the uterus, bladder, and bowel. As the baby grows, these muscles carry more and more weight. Starting Kegel exercises now helps prevent incontinence and can make delivery easier.

**How to do Kegels:** Imagine stopping the flow of urine midstream. Squeeze those muscles, hold for 5 seconds, relax for 5 seconds. Do 3 sets of 10 per day. Your partner can do these anywhere — at her desk, in the car, watching TV. No one will know.
