# Week 25, Day 7

## 👶 Baby Development
As Week 25 comes to a close, your baby is 1.5 pounds of increasingly complex human being. The startle reflex is active, the nostrils are open, the skin is filling out, and the brain is developing at its fastest rate yet. Every day, millions of new neural connections are forming, turning raw brain cells into networks capable of processing the world. Your baby is a sensory being now — hearing, tasting, feeling, balancing, and possibly even smelling. The transformation from a collection of cells to a conscious being is well underway.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Saturday rest is essential. Your partner is closing in on the end of the second trimester, and her body has been working incredibly hard. She might feel the weight of it today — a general heaviness and tiredness that even a good night's sleep doesn't fully resolve. This is normal. The third trimester will bring new challenges, so encourage her to soak in whatever comfort and rest she can find right now. Napping without guilt, reading for pleasure, or simply sitting with a warm cup of tea — all legitimate Saturday activities for a 25-weeks-pregnant woman.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Weekend meal prep idea:** Spend 30 minutes preparing grab-and-go snacks and meals for the week. Suggestions:
- Hard-boil a dozen eggs (protein anytime)
- Wash and cut vegetables for easy snacking
- Make a big batch of hummus
- Portion out trail mix bags (nuts, seeds, dark chocolate, dried fruit)
- Cook a pot of quinoa or rice for quick bowl meals

Having healthy food ready and accessible makes the workweek immeasurably easier, especially when pregnancy fatigue makes cooking feel like a monumental task.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Rest day. A slow walk if she's up for it, gentle stretching if her body is calling for it, or absolutely nothing. The body does remarkable repair work during rest. And for you — use some energy to prepare the week ahead. Clean the house, do the laundry, handle grocery shopping. Physical rest for her, physical productivity for you. Team effort.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's brain has been growing so rapidly that if it continued at this rate outside the womb, it would be the size of a watermelon by the time your baby is one year old. Fortunately, brain growth slows down after birth, but the first year of life is still a period of incredible neural development. By age 3, a child's brain is about 80% of adult size — most of the growth happens before the kid can even string a sentence together.

## 🔮 After-Birth Preview: Breastfeeding — What Nobody Tells You
Breastfeeding is natural, but it doesn't always come naturally. Here's what to actually expect:

**The first few days:**
- Colostrum (thick, golden "first milk") comes in small amounts — this is normal and enough for a newborn's tiny stomach
- Your baby's stomach on day 1 is the size of a cherry (5-7ml). They don't need much
- Getting the "latch" right takes practice for both mom and baby
- It can be uncomfortable or even painful at first. A lactation consultant can make a huge difference

**Your role:**
- Be her encourager. If she wants to breastfeed, support her through the learning curve
- Bring her water (breastfeeding is dehydrating) and snacks during feeds
- Learn to recognize a good latch (baby takes a wide mouthful, not just the nipple)
- If she needs to pump or supplement with formula, don't let anyone make her feel guilty. Fed is best

**If breastfeeding doesn't work out:**
- Formula-fed babies thrive. The science is clear on this
- The best feeding method is the one that works for your family
- Support her decision, whatever it is
