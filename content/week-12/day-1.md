# Week 12, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
This is it — week 12, the grand finale of the first trimester! Your baby is now the size of a lime (~2.1 inches, 5.4 cm) and weighing about 14 grams. This is a monumental week for several reasons:
- Reflexes are developing — your baby can now suck and swallow
- The kidneys are producing urine
- Bone marrow is making white blood cells (immune system building!)
- Fingernails are beginning to form
- Your baby's face looks definitively human
- The intestines are migrating from the umbilical cord back into the abdomen
- The nuchal translucency (NT) scan is usually performed this week
- **The miscarriage risk drops dramatically after this week** — from about 1-5% to less than 1%

For many couples, reaching week 12 feels like exhaling for the first time. The hardest stretch of uncertainty is behind you.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby now has reflexes. Press on your partner's abdomen (gently!) and the baby might wriggle away — though she won't feel it yet. The sucking reflex is developing, which means your baby's lips and mouth are becoming more coordinated. This reflex will be essential for feeding from day one. The digestive system is practicing too: the intestines, which had been looping into the umbilical cord, are now migrating back into the abdominal cavity as the body has grown large enough to contain them. The kidneys are fully functional and producing urine, which becomes part of the amniotic fluid.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Hope. Relief. Still nauseous, maybe, but with a light at the end of the tunnel. Many women find that nausea starts to ease around this week, though it can linger until week 14 or even beyond for some. Your partner's uterus is rising above the pubic bone, which means the doctor might be able to find the heartbeat with a handheld doppler at the next visit. Her appetite might be starting to return — and when it does, the cravings can be intense and specific. Honor them when you can (within reason).

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Mediterranean Salmon Bowl (Full Recipe)**

Omega-3s and protein in one beautiful bowl.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 salmon fillet (about 150g)
- 1 cup cooked brown rice or bulgur
- 1/2 cup cucumber, diced
- 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 2 tbsp hummus
- A few kalamata olives
- Fresh dill or parsley
- Lemon wedge
- 1 tbsp olive oil, salt and pepper

**Steps:**
1. Season salmon with olive oil, salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon.
2. Bake at 200°C (400°F) for 12-15 minutes, or pan-sear for 4 minutes per side.
3. Arrange rice in a bowl. Top with salmon (flaked), cucumber, tomatoes, and olives.
4. Add a generous dollop of hummus.
5. Garnish with fresh herbs and another squeeze of lemon.

Salmon is one of the best sources of DHA (a type of omega-3) for brain development, and it's low in mercury.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Today, try a 20-minute brisk walk. If your partner's energy is starting to return, this is a great week to establish (or re-establish) a regular walking routine. The goal during pregnancy isn't peak fitness — it's consistent, moderate activity. Walking for 30 minutes most days of the week is associated with reduced risk of gestational diabetes, healthier weight gain, better mood, and easier labor. Start with what feels manageable and build from there.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
If the NT scan is scheduled this week, be there. Clear your calendar, rearrange meetings, do whatever you need to do. This is one of the most significant appointments of the pregnancy. You'll see your baby in detail — possibly waving, kicking, or doing somersaults on the screen. Your partner needs your presence, and you don't want to miss this moment. Ask the sonographer to explain what you're seeing. Take mental snapshots.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"After we get through this week's screening, are we ready to tell people? Who do we tell first, and how?" Planning the announcement together can be genuinely fun — and it's a sign that you're moving from anxious early pregnancy into excited, confident parenthood.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can now yawn. Not because it's tired (it sleeps most of the time anyway), but because yawning is a reflex that exercises the jaw muscles and increases blood flow to the brain. It's been observed on ultrasound as early as 11 weeks. Your baby is also making sucking motions, sometimes bringing a thumb or finger to its mouth. Thumb-sucking in the womb is more common than you'd think and is considered a sign of healthy neurological development.

## 👫 Couple Activity
**Create a "Time Capsule" for your baby.** Get a box or envelope and start collecting items: today's newspaper (or a screenshot of today's headlines), a note about what music you're listening to, what shows you're watching, what's happening in the world, the weather today, and a short letter to your future child about this moment in time. Add to it throughout the pregnancy. Open it together on your child's 18th birthday.
