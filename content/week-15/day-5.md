# Week 15, Day 5

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's taste buds are now functional enough to detect different flavors in the amniotic fluid — garlicky, sweet, or savory meals all leave their mark. Baby is also practicing breathing movements, drawing amniotic fluid in and out of the lungs. These aren't real breaths (oxygen comes through the umbilical cord), but it's essential rehearsal for life outside the womb.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby hiccups in the womb! It happens when the diaphragm contracts involuntarily. At this stage the hiccups are tiny and silent, but later in pregnancy the Mom will actually feel rhythmic little jumps in her belly. Some babies hiccup multiple times a day — it's thought to help develop the breathing muscles.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Energy is likely noticeably better than the first trimester — enjoy it! Some women feel so good during weeks 14-17 that they take on extra projects or make big plans. That's great, but rest is still important. The Mom might also notice more vivid dreams — pregnancy hormones combined with lighter sleep (more waking up means more dream recall) make for some truly wild nighttime movies.

## 🤔 Q&A: Yesterday's Answer
Are pregnancy cravings caused by nutritional deficiencies? It's a popular theory, but research doesn't strongly support it. Chocolate is the most commonly craved food in pregnancy, yet it's not a great source of any nutrient a pregnant woman particularly needs. Current thinking is that cravings are driven by hormonal changes, heightened senses, cultural factors, and emotional comfort. That said, some cravings align loosely with needs — craving red meat might connect to iron. The bottom line: cravings are normal, indulging in moderation is fine, and only truly unusual cravings (like wanting to eat dirt or chalk, called "pica") should be discussed with a doctor.

## 🧠 Mental Health Check-In
The second trimester can bring a new kind of anxiety: the "is everything okay in there?" worry. Between appointments, it's natural to feel uncertain. A grounding thought — your baby's body is building itself 24/7 with astonishing precision, and the vast majority of pregnancies progress perfectly normally. If anxiety feels overwhelming, talking to the OB is always a good idea. Prenatal anxiety is common and very treatable.

## 💕 Intimacy
Many couples find that the second trimester brings renewed closeness and desire. The nausea has lifted, energy is back, and the bump isn't yet large enough to feel awkward. Intimacy is completely safe during a normal pregnancy — the baby is well protected by amniotic fluid and the mucus plug. If either of you feels uncertain, that's normal too. There are many ways to be close beyond sex: a foot rub, holding hands, or just lying together and talking.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids (especially DHA) are critical for baby's brain development, which is ramping up fast. Best sources: salmon (aim for 2 servings per week), sardines, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds. If fish isn't appealing, a prenatal DHA supplement is a solid backup. Sprinkle chia seeds on yogurt or oatmeal for a no-effort boost.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
The pelvic tilt is one of the best pregnancy exercises. Lie on your back (still fine at 15 weeks for short periods), knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Flatten the lower back against the floor by gently tightening the abs and tilting the pelvis up. Hold for 5 seconds, release. Do 10 reps. This strengthens the core and lower back, which will be carrying a lot more weight soon.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
A powerful but simple one: ask the Mom "What can I take off your plate this week?" Not "Do you need help?" (which is easy to say no to), but a specific offer to own something entirely — a chore, an errand, a phone call to the insurance company. Completely handling one thing can be more meaningful than a dozen "let me know if you need anything" offers.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
How do you two want to handle visitors after the baby is born? Some couples want all the family there immediately; others want a few days of quiet first. There's no wrong answer, but being on the same page before baby arrives saves a lot of stress.
