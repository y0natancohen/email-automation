# Week 15, Day 5

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's taste buds are now functional enough to detect different flavors in the amniotic fluid. The fluid's taste changes based on what your partner eats — garlicky, sweet, or savory meals all leave their mark. Baby is also practicing breathing movements, drawing amniotic fluid in and out of the lungs. These aren't real breaths (oxygen comes through the umbilical cord), but it's essential rehearsal for life outside the womb. Those tiny legs continue to grow, and your baby is looking more proportional every day.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Energy is likely noticeably better than the first trimester — enjoy it! Some women feel so good during weeks 14-17 that they take on extra projects or make big plans. That's great, but remind your partner that rest is still important. She might also notice more vivid dreams. Pregnancy hormones, combined with lighter sleep (more waking up = more dream recall), make for some truly wild nighttime movies.

## 🤔 Q&A: Yesterday's Answer
**Are pregnancy cravings caused by nutritional deficiencies?** It's a popular theory, but research doesn't strongly support it. For example, chocolate is the most commonly craved food in pregnancy, but it's not a great source of any nutrient a pregnant woman particularly needs. Current thinking is that cravings are driven by a mix of hormonal changes, heightened senses (especially smell), cultural factors, and emotional comfort. That said, some cravings do align loosely with needs — craving red meat might connect to iron needs, for instance. The bottom line: cravings are normal, indulging in moderation is fine, and only truly unusual cravings (like wanting to eat dirt or chalk, called "pica") should be discussed with a doctor.

## 🧠 Mental Health Check-In
The second trimester can bring a new kind of anxiety: the "is everything okay in there?" worry. Between appointments, it's natural to feel uncertain because you can't see or feel the baby yet. Here's a grounding thought — your baby's body is building itself 24/7 with astonishing precision, and the vast majority of pregnancies progress perfectly normally. If anxiety feels overwhelming, talking to the OB about it is always a good idea. Prenatal anxiety is common and very treatable.

## 💕 Intimacy
Good news: many couples find that the second trimester brings a renewed sense of closeness and desire. The nausea has lifted, energy is back, and the bump isn't yet large enough to feel awkward. If your partner is feeling up for it, intimacy is completely safe during a normal pregnancy. The baby is well-protected by the amniotic fluid and the thick mucus plug sealing the cervix. If either of you feels uncertain, that's totally normal too — there are many ways to be intimate beyond sex. Hold hands, give a foot rub, or just lie close together and talk.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids (especially DHA) are critical for baby's brain development, which is ramping up fast. Best sources: salmon (aim for 2 servings per week), sardines, walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds. If fish isn't appealing, a prenatal DHA supplement is a solid backup. Sprinkle chia seeds on yogurt or oatmeal for a no-effort boost.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Let's talk about the **pelvic tilt** — one of the best pregnancy exercises there is. Lie on your back (still fine at 15 weeks for short periods), knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Flatten the lower back against the floor by gently tightening the abs and tilting the pelvis up. Hold for 5 seconds, release. Do 10 reps. This strengthens the core and lower back, which will be carrying a lot more weight in the weeks to come.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's a powerful but simple one: ask your partner "What can I take off your plate this week?" Not "Do you need help?" (which is easy to say no to), but a specific offer to own something entirely — a chore, an errand, a phone call to the insurance company. Taking one thing and completely handling it can be more meaningful than a dozen "let me know if you need anything" offers.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
How do you two want to handle visitors after the baby is born? Some couples want all the family there immediately; others want a few days of quiet first. There's no wrong answer, but being on the same page before the baby arrives saves a lot of stress.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby hiccups in the womb! It happens when the diaphragm contracts involuntarily. At this stage, the hiccups are tiny and silent, but later in pregnancy, your partner will actually feel rhythmic little jumps in her belly. Some babies hiccup multiple times a day — it's thought to help develop the breathing muscles.
