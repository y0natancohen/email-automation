# Week 15, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is having the time of their life in there — rolling, flipping, and doing full somersaults in the amniotic fluid. Think of it like a tiny astronaut in zero gravity. These movements aren't random — they're actually helping develop muscles and coordination. Baby's arms and legs are moving constantly, and those newly hardening bones give the movements more structure every day. Your partner probably can't feel any of this yet (that usually comes a few weeks later for first-time moms), but trust us: it's a party in there.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Increased appetite is very common right now. The second trimester often brings back the desire to eat after weeks of nausea, and the body genuinely needs more fuel — about 300-350 extra calories per day (that's roughly a banana with peanut butter and a glass of milk). Your partner might also notice her skin changing — some women get that famous "pregnancy glow" from increased blood volume, while others deal with breakouts or darkening skin patches (melasma). Both are normal.

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
If your partner is having trouble getting comfortable at night, this is the week to invest in a pregnancy pillow. A full-body C-shaped or U-shaped pillow supports the belly, back, and knees all at once. If that feels like too much pillow, even just a regular pillow between the knees and one behind the back can make a big difference. Also, keeping the bedroom cool (around 65-68°F / 18-20°C) helps with the temperature regulation issues that come with increased blood volume.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If your partner hasn't shared her pregnancy news at work yet, week 15 is often when women start to feel more comfortable making the announcement — the first trimester risk period is well past, and the bump is getting harder to hide. Encourage her to think about who to tell first (usually a direct manager) and when feels right. There's no perfect time, but having a loose plan can reduce the stress of "when do I say something?"

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Iron needs increase significantly during pregnancy. A simple **Iron-Boosting Lunch Bowl**: cook up some quinoa, top with sauteed spinach, chickpeas, sliced red bell peppers, and a squeeze of lemon. The vitamin C from the peppers and lemon helps the body absorb the plant-based iron much better. Add some feta if she's into cheese (pasteurized only!).

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Kegel exercises — they're not glamorous, but they're incredibly important. Strong pelvic floor muscles help during delivery and recovery. The basic move: squeeze the muscles you'd use to stop peeing midstream, hold for 5 seconds, release for 5 seconds, repeat 10 times. Do three sets throughout the day. Your partner can do these absolutely anywhere — at her desk, on the couch, in the car. Nobody will know.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Your partner's appetite is likely back in full force. Surprise her with her current craving — whether that's a specific takeout dish, a particular snack, or something homemade. It's a small gesture that says "I'm paying attention to what you need." Bonus points if you have it ready before she even asks.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
Where do you want your baby to sleep for the first few months? In your room in a bassinet? In the nursery from the start? Co-sleeping? There's no single right answer, but it's good to start talking about it now so you can plan the space.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At 15 weeks, your baby is developing a unique set of fingerprints. These form based on the baby's movements and position in the womb, which means they're influenced by the amniotic fluid environment. Even identical twins have different fingerprints because of this!
