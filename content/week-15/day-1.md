# Week 15, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to week 15! Your baby is about the size of an apple — roughly 4 inches from crown to rump. This week is all about movement and senses. Your little one can actually sense light through closed eyelids, bones are hardening from soft cartilage into real bone, and those legs are growing longer than the arms now. Baby is busy in there — rolling, flipping, and doing somersaults your partner probably can't feel yet. Taste buds are also forming, meaning your baby is already getting a preview of flavors through the amniotic fluid. This is one active week!

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is about 4 inches long — picture a crisp apple sitting in the palm of your hand. One of the coolest developments this week: your baby can now sense light. Even though those tiny eyelids are still sealed shut, if your partner shines a flashlight at her belly, baby might actually turn away from it. The skeleton is going through ossification — a fancy word meaning cartilage is turning into real bone. You'd also notice (if you could peek in) that the legs have finally outgrown the arms, making baby look more proportional.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The second trimester is often called the "honeymoon period" of pregnancy, and many women start feeling noticeably better around now. Morning sickness is likely fading (finally!), and energy levels are picking up. Your partner might notice her nose feels stuffy — pregnancy rhinitis is super common thanks to increased blood flow. She may also be starting to show more, and those maternity jeans are becoming less of a "maybe" and more of a "must."

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Calcium is huge right now as baby's bones are hardening. Your partner needs about 1,000 mg daily. Here's a quick **Calcium-Packed Smoothie**: blend 1 cup yogurt, a handful of spinach, 1 banana, 1 tbsp almond butter, and a splash of fortified orange juice. It tastes like a treat but delivers a serious calcium punch. If she's not into dairy, fortified plant milks, tofu, and sardines (if she's adventurous) are great alternatives.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Swimming or water aerobics is one of the best exercises during the second trimester. The buoyancy takes pressure off joints and the growing belly, while still providing a full-body workout. Even just walking laps in the pool counts. If there's no pool nearby, a 20-minute walk outside works wonders for both energy and mood.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
This is a great week to start talking to your baby. Yes, it might feel a little awkward at first, but hearing starts developing soon, and getting into the habit now means it'll feel natural by the time baby can actually hear you. Lean in close to your partner's belly and just say hi. Tell baby about your day. Read a book out loud. It's a bonding moment for all three of you.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
Talk about your baby's name list tonight. Have either of you been secretly adding names? Share your top three picks with each other — no vetoing allowed yet, just listening. You might be surprised by what the other person loves.

## 👫 Couple Activity
**Apple Taste Test!** Since your baby is the size of an apple this week, grab 3-4 different apple varieties from the store — Fuji, Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, Gala — slice them up and do a blind taste test together. Rate them, pick a favorite. Silly? Absolutely. Fun? Definitely. Bonus: apples are packed with fiber and vitamin C.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby is producing urine now — and yes, peeing into the amniotic fluid, then swallowing it, then peeing again. It sounds gross, but it's actually a crucial part of kidney and digestive system development. The ultimate recycling program!
