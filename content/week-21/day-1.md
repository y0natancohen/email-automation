# Week 21, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to Week 21 — you're officially past the halfway mark! Your baby is about 10.5 inches long (crown to heel — from this week on, we measure head to toe instead of head to rump, which is why the number jumped!) and weighs around 12 ounces, roughly the size of a large banana. This week is all about coordination: your baby's movements are becoming more deliberate and purposeful, less like random twitches and more like a tiny person testing out their body. The bone marrow is stepping up to take over blood cell production (previously handled by the liver and spleen — teamwork!). Eyebrows and eyelids are fully formed, and here's the coolest part: your baby is swallowing amniotic fluid regularly and can actually taste traces of what your partner eats. So if she had garlic bread last night, your baby got a little preview. This week, we'll focus on movement milestones, nutrition for brain growth, and how you can stay connected to the journey as things start feeling very real.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's movements are getting more coordinated this week. Instead of random flutters, there's actual purpose — stretching, turning, even somersaulting. The bone marrow is now producing red blood cells, a major milestone in your baby's development. Those tiny eyebrows and eyelids? Fully developed now, giving your baby a much more recognizable face. And every time your partner eats, the flavors pass into the amniotic fluid, which your baby swallows. That's right — your little one is already forming taste preferences!

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might be hitting a sweet spot right now. The rough first trimester is a distant memory, and she's not yet in the heavy-lifting third trimester. She may notice her belly growing more rapidly, and round ligament pain (sharp twinges on the sides of the belly) can pop up with sudden movements. Some women get leg cramps at night around this time, and increased appetite is very normal — she's feeding a growing human, after all. Emotionally, this can be a confident, energized period. Enjoy it together.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Iron-Rich Lentil Soup (Full Recipe)**

Your baby's bone marrow needs iron to produce all those new blood cells. This hearty soup is packed with it.

*Ingredients:*
- 1.5 cups red lentils, rinsed
- 1 onion, diced
- 2 carrots, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can diced tomatoes
- 6 cups vegetable broth
- 1 tsp cumin, 1/2 tsp turmeric
- Squeeze of lemon juice
- Fresh parsley

*Directions:*
1. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil until soft (5 min)
2. Add carrots, spices, cook 1 minute
3. Add lentils, tomatoes, broth. Bring to boil
4. Simmer 25 minutes until lentils are tender
5. Blend half for creamy texture, stir back in
6. Finish with lemon juice and parsley

Iron absorption tip: The lemon juice boosts iron uptake thanks to vitamin C.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
A 20-minute walk after dinner is one of the best things your partner can do right now. It helps with digestion, reduces leg cramps, and keeps circulation flowing. Bonus: walk together and use it as dedicated talk time. If she's feeling energetic, a prenatal yoga session focused on hip openers can help prepare the body for what's ahead.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start paying attention to when you feel the baby move. Put your hand on your partner's belly during quiet moments — evenings on the couch, mornings in bed. Your baby is getting strong enough that you might feel kicks from the outside soon. These moments bond you to the pregnancy in a way nothing else can.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can now taste the difference between sweet and bitter flavors in the amniotic fluid. Studies show babies swallow more when the fluid is sweet and less when it's bitter. Tiny food critics in the making!
