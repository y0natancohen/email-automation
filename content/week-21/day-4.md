# Week 21, Day 4

## 👶 Baby Development
A major behind-the-scenes milestone is happening: your baby's bone marrow is now the primary producer of red blood cells. Up until recently, the liver and spleen were handling this job, but the bone marrow is stepping up and taking over for good. This is a sign of maturing bones and a more adult-like blood system. Meanwhile, your baby continues to practice coordinated movements — flexing fingers, curling toes, and moving limbs with increasing purpose.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner's belly button might be starting to pop outward — this is completely normal and happens as the growing uterus pushes the belly forward. She may also notice changes in her hair: many women experience thicker, fuller hair during pregnancy thanks to hormones slowing the normal hair shedding cycle. Enjoy it while it lasts — postpartum shedding is real! Energy levels are often good during the second trimester, but occasional fatigue can still hit, especially after busy days.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Folate-Rich Mediterranean Salad (Full Recipe)**

Folate remains important for baby's continued neural development.

*Ingredients:*
- 2 cups fresh spinach
- 1/2 cup cooked chickpeas
- 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
- 1/2 avocado, sliced
- Cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
- Handful of fresh mint leaves

*Dressing:*
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp za'atar
- Salt and pepper to taste

*Directions:*
Toss everything together. Drizzle dressing on top. Done in 5 minutes.

This salad hits folate (spinach), healthy fats (avocado, olive oil), protein (chickpeas, feta), and flavor (za'atar and mint).

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Cat-Cow Stretch for Back Relief:**
1. Get on all fours, hands under shoulders, knees under hips
2. Inhale: drop belly, lift head and tailbone (Cow)
3. Exhale: round spine, tuck chin and tailbone (Cat)
4. Move slowly between positions for 1 minute
5. Repeat 3 times throughout the day

This is one of the best exercises for the back pain that comes with a growing belly. It gently mobilizes the spine and strengthens the core.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start reading to the belly. Yes, it feels a little weird at first, but your baby is developing the ability to hear, and your voice — with its lower frequency — actually travels well through the amniotic fluid. Pick a children's book and read a page or two each night. By the time your baby is born, they'll recognize the rhythm of your voice. Some dads pick one book and read it throughout the pregnancy so it becomes the baby's "comfort story."

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's bone marrow will produce about 200 billion red blood cells per day by adulthood. Right now, it's just getting started, but this tiny factory is already ramping up production. Each red blood cell will travel the entire circulatory system in about 20 seconds.

## 🌍 Cultural & Science Deep-Dive: The Science of Fetal Taste
Research from the Monell Chemical Senses Center has shown that flavors from a mother's diet appear in amniotic fluid within 1-2 hours of eating. Babies exposed to diverse flavors in the womb tend to be more accepting of varied foods after birth. In cultures with strong culinary traditions — from Indian spices to Japanese umami — babies essentially get an early introduction to their culture's cuisine. This is one reason why pediatric nutritionists encourage pregnant women to eat a diverse, flavorful diet. Your partner isn't just nourishing the baby — she's introducing them to the family's food culture.
