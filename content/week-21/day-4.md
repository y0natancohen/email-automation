# Week 21, Day 4

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's bone marrow will produce about 200 billion red blood cells per day by adulthood. Right now it's just getting started, but this tiny factory is already ramping up. Each red blood cell will travel the entire circulatory system in about 20 seconds.

## 👶 Baby Development
A major behind-the-scenes milestone is happening: your baby's bone marrow is now the primary producer of red blood cells. The liver and spleen handled this job until recently, but the bone marrow is taking over for good — a sign of maturing bones and a more adult-like blood system. Meanwhile, your baby continues practicing coordinated movements, flexing fingers, curling toes, and moving limbs with increasing purpose.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom's belly button might be starting to pop outward — completely normal as the growing uterus pushes everything forward. She may also notice thicker, fuller hair thanks to hormones slowing the normal shedding cycle (enjoy it while it lasts — postpartum shedding is real). Energy levels are often good during the second trimester, but occasional fatigue can still hit after busy days.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Folate remains important for your baby's continued neural development. A quick Mediterranean salad covers a lot of ground: toss fresh spinach with chickpeas, crumbled feta, avocado, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and fresh mint, then drizzle with olive oil, lemon juice, and za'atar. Done in five minutes, and it delivers folate, healthy fats, protein, and flavor all in one bowl.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
The cat-cow stretch is one of the best exercises for the back pain that comes with a growing belly. On all fours, alternate between dropping the belly while lifting the head (cow) and rounding the spine while tucking the chin (cat). Move slowly between positions for about a minute, and repeat a few times throughout the day. It gently mobilizes the spine and strengthens the core.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start reading to the belly. It feels a little weird at first, but your baby is developing hearing, and your lower-frequency voice travels well through the amniotic fluid. Pick a children's book and read a page or two each night. By the time your baby is born, they'll recognize the rhythm of your voice. Some dads pick one book and read it throughout the pregnancy — it becomes the baby's comfort story.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"Let's pick a children's book to read together throughout the rest of the pregnancy. What book meant something to you as a kid?"

## 🌍 The Science of Fetal Taste
Research from the Monell Chemical Senses Center has shown that flavors from the Mom's diet appear in amniotic fluid within one to two hours of eating. Babies exposed to diverse flavors in the womb tend to be more accepting of varied foods after birth. In cultures with strong culinary traditions — from Indian spices to Japanese umami — babies essentially get an early introduction to their cuisine. This is one reason pediatric nutritionists encourage a diverse, flavorful diet during pregnancy.
