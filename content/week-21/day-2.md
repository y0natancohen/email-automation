# Week 21, Day 2

## 👶 Baby Development
Let's talk about your baby's face — it's looking more and more like a real person every day. Those eyebrows? Fully formed. The eyelids are complete too, though they'll stay shut for a few more weeks. Underneath, the eyes are developing rapidly. Your baby's movements today are a mix of kicks, stretches, and the occasional hiccup. The digestive system is practicing too, as your baby continues to swallow amniotic fluid and process it through the intestines.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might notice her skin is doing interesting things. Some women get the famous "pregnancy glow" — thanks to increased blood volume giving the skin a flushed, dewy look. Others might deal with breakouts, dry patches, or darkening skin (especially a line down the belly called the linea nigra). Stretch marks might start appearing on the belly, breasts, or hips. All of this is completely normal and temporary. She might also feel warmer than usual — extra blood volume means extra body heat.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Calcium spotlight:** Your baby's bones are hardening, and that takes calcium. If your partner isn't a big milk drinker, no worries — calcium is in tahini, sardines, fortified orange juice, broccoli, almonds, and white beans. A quick snack idea: apple slices with almond butter and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Tasty, easy, and calcium-packed.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Monday Movement Focus: Pelvic Floor Strengtheners**

The pelvic floor is the unsung hero of pregnancy. Strong pelvic floor muscles support the growing uterus, help with labor, and speed up postpartum recovery.

**Kegel Exercise Routine:**
1. Identify the muscles by imagining stopping the flow of urine (don't actually do this while urinating)
2. Contract and hold for 5 seconds, then release for 5 seconds
3. Repeat 10 times
4. Do 3 sets throughout the day

**Bridge Pose:**
1. Lie on back with knees bent, feet flat on floor
2. Squeeze glutes and pelvic floor, lift hips
3. Hold 5 seconds, lower slowly
4. Repeat 10 times

**Tip for you:** Learn these exercises too. Pelvic floor strength matters for everyone, and doing them together makes it less awkward and more of a shared routine.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
If your partner is dealing with skin changes, be genuinely reassuring — not dismissive. "You look great" is nice but vague. Try something specific: "I love how you look right now. You're growing our baby and it shows in the best way." Specificity feels real. Also, if she's interested, a gentle belly moisturizing routine together can be a nice nightly ritual.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At 21 weeks, your baby's fingerprints are forming. The swirling patterns on those tiny fingertips are being created by the pressure of amniotic fluid on the developing skin. No two people — not even identical twins — share the same fingerprints.

## 🤔 Q&A: Yesterday's Answer
**Can babies hear music in the womb?**
Yes! By week 21, your baby's hearing is developing rapidly. While it's not fully mature yet (that comes closer to week 25-26), your baby can detect sounds, especially low-frequency ones. Studies have shown that newborns recognize music and voices they heard frequently during pregnancy. The rhythmic, low-frequency sounds — like bass lines in music or a father's deep voice — travel particularly well through amniotic fluid. So yes, play music, talk to the belly, sing off-key. Your baby is listening, and they'll remember.
