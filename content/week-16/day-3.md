# Week 16, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
The nervous system is making major leaps this week. Nerve cells are multiplying rapidly, and the connections between brain and muscles are getting faster and stronger. This means your baby's movements are becoming more refined — less like random jerks and more like intentional stretches and reaches. The facial expressions keep developing too. Baby can now frown, squint, and even look like they're concentrating. These aren't emotions yet — just the nervous system running diagnostics — but they're laying the groundwork for the expressive little face you'll fall in love with.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might experience some dizziness when standing up quickly. This happens because blood vessels are more relaxed during pregnancy (thanks, progesterone), and it takes a moment for blood pressure to adjust with position changes. The fix is simple: stand up slowly, especially from lying down. First sit, pause, then stand. She might also notice her appetite is strong and consistent — this is the body's way of saying "feed me, I'm building a human."

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
Leg cramps at night are a common second-trimester annoyance. They often strike the calves and can jolt your partner awake. Prevention strategies: stretch the calves before bed (stand on a step, let heels hang off, hold for 30 seconds), drink enough water throughout the day, and eat magnesium-rich foods like bananas and almonds. If a cramp hits, gently flex the foot (toes toward the shin, not pointed away) and massage the muscle. Keeping a small pillow or folded blanket near the bed to prop the feet up slightly can also help.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If your partner has announced her pregnancy at work, now's a good time to start thinking about maternity leave logistics — not the emotional stuff, just the practical. When does she plan to start leave? What projects will need handover? Is there a colleague who can cover? Having a rough plan early makes the transition much smoother and less stressful than scrambling at the end. For you, look into paternity leave options too — understanding your rights and your employer's policy is best done now, not when you're sleep-deprived in month 9.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Protein needs increase during pregnancy — your partner needs about 75-100 grams per day. A **Quick Greek Yogurt Protein Bowl**: a cup of plain Greek yogurt (18g protein right there), topped with granola, sliced almonds, berries, and a drizzle of honey. Easy breakfast or snack that packs a protein punch without any cooking.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Squats** are one of the most functional pregnancy exercises. They strengthen the legs and glutes, open the hips, and can even help with labor. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out. Slowly lower down as if sitting into a chair, keeping weight in the heels and chest lifted. Go as deep as comfortable, then stand back up. Start with 2 sets of 10. Hold onto a chair or counter for balance if needed.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Look into childbirth education classes in your area — many hospitals and community centers offer them, and some are available online. These classes cover what to expect during labor, pain management options, and how you can support your partner during delivery. Classes fill up, so booking around weeks 16-20 for classes that happen around weeks 28-32 is smart planning.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
Who would you want as your baby's godparent or honorary uncle/aunt? Not necessarily religious — just someone you'd trust to be a meaningful presence in your child's life. It's a fun and sometimes surprisingly deep conversation.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's eyes are slowly moving from the sides of the head toward the front. Right now, they're still a bit wide-set, but by birth they'll be in their final position. This migration is part of why early ultrasound images can look a little alien-like — the features are all there, they're just still arranging themselves!
