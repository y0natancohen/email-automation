# Week 32, Day 6

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby now has fully formed toenails! These tiny nails have been growing gradually, and by this point they've reached the tips of the toes. Fingernails have been there for a while, but toenails take a bit longer because the feet develop slightly behind the hands. Meanwhile, bones throughout the body continue to harden through a process called ossification — calcium and phosphorus are being deposited into the cartilage framework, turning it into solid bone. The one important exception? The skull. Those skull plates remain separated by flexible sutures and fontanelles, allowing the head to compress slightly during birth. Your baby weighs about 3.75 pounds and is roughly the size of a jicama.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Shortness of breath is increasingly common as the uterus pushes up against the diaphragm. Your partner might find herself winded after climbing stairs or even talking for extended periods. This is completely normal — her lungs are working fine, they just have less room to expand. Sleeping propped up on pillows can help at night. She might also notice that the baby's movements feel different — less like kicks and flutters and more like rolls and stretches, because there's simply less room in there for acrobatics.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Shabbat Recipe: Lemon Herb Salmon with Roasted Sweet Potatoes**

This is packed with omega-3 fatty acids (critical for baby's brain development) and beta-carotene (from the sweet potatoes, which supports baby's skin and eye development).

*For the salmon:*
- 2 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 lemon, juiced and zested
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
- 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper to taste

*For the sweet potatoes:*
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon cumin
- Salt and pepper

*Instructions:*
1. Preheat oven to 400°F/200°C
2. Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes
3. While potatoes roast, mix olive oil, lemon juice, zest, garlic, dill, and parsley. Place salmon on a separate baking sheet lined with parchment paper and pour the lemon herb mixture over the fillets
4. After 20 minutes, add the salmon to the oven alongside the sweet potatoes. Bake both for 12-15 minutes until salmon flakes easily
5. Serve together with a simple green salad

Serves 2. The omega-3s in salmon are especially important during the third trimester when your baby's brain is in its most intense growth period.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Time to get strategic about the hospital bag. You've probably seen lists, but here's what actually matters — the things people forget or wish they'd thought of:

**Must-buy items you might not have:**
- **A long phone charging cable** (hospital outlets are never where you need them)
- **Lip balm** (hospitals are dry, and during labor, lips get very dry from breathing)
- **A small portable speaker** (for your labor playlist — phone speakers don't cut it)
- **Dark-colored towel from home** (for postpartum, because hospital towels are small and white)
- **Nipple cream** (lanolin-based — your partner will thank you on day 1)
- **Mesh underwear or disposable postpartum underwear** (the hospital provides some, but extras are smart)
- **A pillow from home** (hospital pillows are flat and uncomfortable — bring one with a colored pillowcase so it doesn't get mixed up)
- **Snacks that don't need refrigeration** (granola bars, dried fruit, nuts, crackers with peanut butter packets)
- **A going-home outfit for the baby** in newborn AND 0-3 month size (you don't know how big the baby will be)

Go pick up whatever you're missing this weekend. Having the bag fully packed and by the door removes one major source of anxiety.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's toenails are made of keratin — the same protein that makes up your hair, fingernails, and the outer layer of your skin. Keratin is one of the toughest biological materials in nature. It's what makes rhino horns, bird beaks, and horse hooves so strong. Your baby is literally growing the same tough stuff that protects some of the world's most resilient animals. After birth, those tiny toenails will need trimming surprisingly soon — they grow fast!

## 💰 Financial Tip
Hospital bag shopping doesn't have to break the bank. Here's a budget-smart approach:
- **Check what the hospital provides.** Call the labor and delivery unit and ask specifically what they supply (diapers, wipes, pads, mesh underwear, baby blankets). Many Israeli hospitals provide more than you'd expect.
- **Don't buy the "hospital bag kits"** sold online — they're marked up and contain things you don't need.
- **Buy generic.** The baby doesn't care about brand names. Generic diapers and wipes work perfectly well for the first days.
- **Total budget for hospital bag supplies:** You should be able to fill in the gaps for 150-250 NIS if you shop smart. The big items (car seat, stroller) are separate purchases you've hopefully already made.

## 🧘 Mindfulness Moment
Tonight, before Shabbat, try the "arrival meditation." Stand at your front door for 60 seconds before entering the house. Leave the workweek stress on the doorstep. Take three breaths: the first breath releases what happened today, the second breath brings you into the present moment, and the third breath sets your intention to be fully present with your partner tonight. Then walk through the door as if you're arriving somewhere new — because in a way, every evening together at this stage is a new chapter.

## 🕯️ Shabbat Activity
**Hospital Bag Packing Sprint:** Set a 30-minute timer and pack the hospital bags together. Put on music, pour some (non-alcoholic) drinks, and make it fun. One bag for her (comfort items, toiletries, going-home outfit, nursing supplies), one bag for you (change of clothes, snacks, entertainment, chargers), and one for the baby (outfit, swaddle, car seat — stays in the car). When the timer goes off, stop. Whatever isn't packed, write a quick list of what's still needed. The goal is progress, not perfection. Having the bags 80% packed and sitting by the door will make you both sleep better tonight.
