# Week 32, Day 6

## 👶 Baby Development
Those tiny toenails have reached the tips of the toes. Fingernails arrived earlier, but toenails take a bit longer since the feet develop slightly behind the hands. Bones throughout the body continue to harden as calcium and phosphorus are deposited into the cartilage framework, turning it into solid bone. Your baby weighs about 3.75 pounds and is roughly the size of a jicama.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Shortness of breath is increasingly common as the uterus presses up against the diaphragm. The Mom might find herself winded after climbing stairs or even talking for extended periods — her lungs are working fine, they just have less room to expand. Sleeping propped up on pillows can help at night. She may also notice the baby's movements feel different now — less like kicks and flutters, more like rolls and stretches, because there's simply less room for acrobatics.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids are especially important during the third trimester, when the baby's brain is in its most intense growth period. A simple meal idea: lemon herb salmon with roasted sweet potatoes. Marinate salmon fillets in olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and fresh dill, then bake at 200C for about 12-15 minutes alongside cubed sweet potatoes tossed with smoked paprika and cumin. The sweet potatoes add beta-carotene for skin and eye development. Simple, nourishing, and done in under 40 minutes.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Time to get strategic about the hospital bag. Beyond the obvious items, here's what people actually forget or wish they'd packed: a long phone charging cable (hospital outlets are never where you need them), lip balm (hospitals are dry, and labor makes lips even drier), a small portable speaker for the playlist, a dark-colored towel from home, nipple cream, a pillow from home with a colored pillowcase so it doesn't get mixed up with hospital pillows, and non-perishable snacks. Also pack a going-home outfit for the baby in both newborn and 0-3 month sizes, since you won't know the baby's size in advance.

## 💬 Conversation Starter

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's toenails are made of keratin — the same protein that forms hair, fingernails, and the outer layer of skin. Keratin is one of the toughest biological materials in nature — it's what makes rhino horns, bird beaks, and horse hooves so strong. After birth, those tiny nails will need trimming surprisingly soon.

## 💰 Financial Tip
Hospital bag shopping doesn't have to be expensive. Call the labor and delivery unit first and ask what they supply — many Israeli hospitals provide more than you'd expect (diapers, wipes, pads, mesh underwear, baby blankets). Skip the pre-packaged "hospital bag kits" sold online — they're marked up and full of things you don't need. Buy generic for the basics. Total budget to fill in the gaps: roughly 150-250 NIS if you shop smart.

## 🕯️ Shabbat Activity
Set a 30-minute timer and pack the hospital bags together. Put on music, make it fun. One bag for her, one for you, one for the baby (outfit, swaddle, car seat stays in the car). When the timer goes off, stop — whatever isn't packed, jot down a quick list. The goal is progress, not perfection. Having bags 80% packed and by the door will help you both sleep better tonight.
