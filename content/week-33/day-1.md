# Week 33, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Your baby is about 17.2 inches long and weighs around 4.25 pounds — roughly the size of a pineapple. This week's big story: your baby's immune system is getting a serious upgrade. Antibodies are being transferred from your partner through the placenta, giving the baby a "starter pack" of immunity against diseases mom has encountered or been vaccinated against. The pupils can now constrict and dilate in response to light changes. Bones are continuing to harden (skull still flexible). The brain is growing at a staggering rate, and the baby is settling into more defined sleep cycles, including REM sleep — meaning your baby is dreaming. Seven weeks to go!

## 👶 Baby Development
The immune system transfer happening right now is one of nature's most elegant solutions. Your partner's body is packaging up antibodies — proteins that recognize and fight specific pathogens — and sending them across the placenta into the baby's bloodstream. These are called IgG antibodies, and they provide passive immunity that will protect the baby for the first few months of life while their own immune system learns to produce its own defenses. This is why the timing of certain vaccines during pregnancy (like the whooping cough booster and flu shot) matters — the antibodies generated by those vaccines get passed directly to the baby. Your baby is literally borrowing your partner's immune experience.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
At 33 weeks, your partner's uterus is now about 5 inches above the belly button. She may feel like her ribcage is being pushed outward (it is — the ribs actually expand during pregnancy to accommodate the growing uterus). Heartburn might be especially intense as the stomach gets compressed. Sleeping positions are increasingly limited — left side is still recommended for optimal blood flow. She may also notice more frequent Braxton Hicks contractions, which are practice contractions that feel like a tightening across the belly. They're normal, but if they become regular or painful, it's worth calling the doctor.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Immune-Boosting Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry**

Since we're talking about immunity this week, here's a recipe loaded with immune-supporting nutrients — zinc from chicken, vitamin C from bell peppers, and antioxidants from garlic and ginger.

*Ingredients:*
- 2 chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup broccoli florets
- 1 cup snap peas
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce (low sodium)
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Cooked rice or noodles for serving
- Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

*Instructions:*
1. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat
2. Add chicken strips and cook until golden, about 4-5 minutes. Remove and set aside
3. In the same pan, add garlic and ginger. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant
4. Add bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until crisp-tender
5. Mix soy sauce, sesame oil, and honey in a small bowl. Pour over vegetables
6. Return chicken to the pan. Add cornstarch mixture and stir until sauce thickens, about 1 minute
7. Serve over rice or noodles. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions

Serves 2-3. Quick, colorful, and exactly what a third-trimester body needs. Leftovers make a great lunch the next day.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
The antibodies crossing the placenta right now are incredibly specific. If your partner had chickenpox as a child, antibodies against chickenpox are being transferred to the baby. If she was vaccinated against measles, measles antibodies are being sent over. This borrowed immunity typically lasts 3-6 months after birth, which is why the first round of baby vaccinations is scheduled to begin as the maternal antibodies start to wane. It's like a relay race — mom carries the baton for the first few months, then the baby's own immune system takes over.
