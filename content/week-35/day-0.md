# Week 35, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Week 35. Your baby is about 18.2 inches long and weighs approximately 5.25 pounds. Most physical development is now complete — from here on, the focus is weight gain, about half a pound per week. The kidneys are fully developed, the liver can process waste products, and your little one is putting on the finishing touches. Movements may feel different because there's less room for somersaults, but regular activity should continue. This is also the week to start thinking about the Group B Strep (GBS) test, which typically happens between weeks 35 and 37.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby already has unique fingerprints — they formed way back around weeks 17-20. Those prints are permanent, and no one else in the world will ever share them.

## 👶 Baby Development
The kidneys are fully mature and producing urine that contributes to amniotic fluid. The liver is also developed enough to process some waste products on its own. Arms and legs are getting plumper by the day as fat continues to build. Hearing is so refined now that your baby can distinguish between different voices and may respond to familiar songs or stories.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom might notice that movements feel different — less like dramatic kicks and more like rolls, stretches, and pushes. This is simply because there's less room. Movements should still be regular and consistent; if she notices a significant decrease, she should contact her care provider. Braxton Hicks may be more frequent, and pelvic pressure continues to build.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids, especially DHA, remain crucial for brain development in these final weeks. Great sources: salmon (two to three servings per week is safe), walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds. If the Mom isn't eating much fish, a DHA supplement is worth discussing with the doctor. For a fiber-rich meal, try a warm lentil and roasted vegetable salad: roast butternut squash and broccoli with smoked paprika, toss with cooked lentils, and drizzle with a lemon-tahini dressing. Lentils provide about 16 grams of fiber per cup, plus iron and protein.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Birth ball exercises are fantastic at this stage. Sitting on the ball helps open the pelvis and encourages the baby to move into the optimal head-down position. Try gentle bouncing, hip circles, and figure-eight movements. Even just sitting on the ball instead of the couch while watching TV counts as beneficial.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Confirm your route to the hospital this week. Actually drive it at different times of day. Know where the entrance is, where you'll park, and where labor and delivery is located. Some couples do a dry run at night to time it. It sounds excessive until you're driving at 2 AM with a laboring partner and realize you've never actually been to this entrance before.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What names are still on the table? Should we have our final name conversation this week?" If you've already settled, maybe discuss middle names or whether to share the name before birth.

## 🤔 Q&A Question
What is Group B Strep (GBS), and should you be worried about the test? Answer coming tomorrow — it's simpler than you might think.

## 👫 Couple Activity
Do a baby gear check together. Pull out the car seat, stroller, and any other big items and make sure everything is assembled, clean, and functional. Practice opening and closing the stroller — it's harder than it looks. Check that the car seat is properly installed, or schedule an inspection this week. Put on some music and make it a team effort.
