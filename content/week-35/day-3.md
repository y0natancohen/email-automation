# Week 35, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Baby's fat stores are increasing rapidly — this isn't just about looking chubby (though that's a cute bonus). The fat serves two critical purposes: insulation for temperature regulation after birth, and energy reserves for the first days of life before breastfeeding is fully established. Your baby's skin is becoming smoother and less wrinkled as these fat deposits fill out underneath.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Edema (swelling) in the feet, ankles, and hands might be at its worst. Rings might be tight, and shoes might feel small. This is due to increased blood volume and fluid retention. If swelling is sudden or severe — especially in the face — or accompanied by headache or vision changes, call the care provider immediately as this could be a sign of preeclampsia. Normal swelling, while annoying, is harmless and will resolve after birth.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Quick Energy Recipe: No-Bake Date Energy Balls**

- 1 cup pitted dates
- 1/2 cup oats
- 1/4 cup almond butter
- 2 tbsp cocoa powder
- 2 tbsp chia seeds
- 1 tbsp honey
- Pinch of salt

Blend dates in a food processor until a paste forms. Mix in remaining ingredients. Roll into small balls. Refrigerate for 30 minutes. Makes about 15 balls. Perfect grab-and-go snack for now and for the hospital bag — packed with iron, fiber, and natural energy. They also freeze well.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
If your partner is dealing with swollen feet and ankles, elevating the legs for 15-20 minutes several times a day can help. Ankle circles and foot pumps (pointing toes up and down) while lying down improve circulation. Avoid standing for long periods, and when sitting, keep feet up on a stool. Compression socks can also be surprisingly effective.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start preparing freezer meals. This is one of the most practical things you can do. Cook double portions of soups, stews, casseroles, and pasta sauces and freeze them in labeled containers. When baby arrives and cooking feels impossible, you'll pull out a home-cooked meal in minutes. Aim for at least 7-10 meals in the freezer by your due date. Even if cooking isn't your thing — canned soup portioned into containers counts. No judgment.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What are we most looking forward to about the first year? What milestone are you most excited for?" First smile? First laugh? First steps? Dreaming about the good stuff counterbalances the anxiety.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies practice "breathing" in the womb by inhaling and exhaling amniotic fluid. Occasionally, they get hiccups from this — and you can feel them! Fetal hiccups feel like small, rhythmic pulses, different from kicks. They can last for 15-20 minutes and may happen multiple times a day.

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
**The 3 AM Wakeup Strategy:** If your partner keeps waking up at 3 AM and can't fall back asleep, instead of lying there frustrated, try this routine: bathroom break, sip of water, 5 minutes of gentle stretching or slow breathing, then back to bed with a boring podcast or audiobook (not a screen). The key is accepting wakefulness without fighting it — resistance creates more tension. Also: a banana before bed can help, as it contains tryptophan and magnesium, both of which promote sleep.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
For you: make sure your workplace knows you might need to leave suddenly in the coming weeks. Have a plan for your most important tasks — who takes over what, where key files are, and how to reach you in an emergency. Set up an out-of-office message template now so you can activate it with one click. If you have meetings or deadlines in the next 5 weeks, start thinking about contingency plans. The peace of mind from being professionally prepared is worth the 30 minutes of planning.
