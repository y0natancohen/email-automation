# Week 35, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Week 35! Your baby is about 18.2 inches long and weighs approximately 5.25 pounds. Here's the exciting part: most physical development is now complete. From here on out, baby is mostly focused on gaining weight — about half a pound per week. The kidneys are fully developed, the liver can process waste products, and your little one is essentially just putting on the finishing touches. Movements might feel different because there's less room to do somersaults, but you should still feel regular activity. This is also the week to start thinking about the Group B Strep (GBS) test, which typically happens between weeks 35-37.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's kidneys are now fully developed and producing urine that contributes to the amniotic fluid. The liver is also mature enough to process some waste products on its own. Baby's arms and legs are getting plumper by the day as fat continues to accumulate. The hearing is so refined now that baby can distinguish between different voices and may even respond to familiar songs or stories.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might notice that baby's movements feel different — less like dramatic kicks and more like rolls, stretches, and pushes. This is because there's simply less room. But the movements should still be regular and consistent. If she notices a significant decrease, she should contact her care provider. She may also be experiencing Braxton Hicks more frequently, and the pelvic pressure continues to build.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Omega-3 fatty acids (especially DHA) are crucial for baby's brain development in these final weeks. Great sources: fatty fish like salmon (2-3 servings per week is safe), walnuts, chia seeds, and flaxseeds. If your partner isn't eating much fish, talk to the care provider about a DHA supplement. A quick snack idea: mix chia seeds into yogurt with a drizzle of honey.

**Warm Lentil & Roasted Vegetable Salad with Lemon-Tahini Dressing**

With baby's kidneys now fully developed and processing waste, fiber-rich meals help mom's digestive system keep up with the extra demands of late pregnancy. This hearty salad is packed with fiber and keeps everything moving.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 cup cooked green or brown lentils
- 1 cup diced butternut squash
- 1 cup broccoli florets
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp tahini
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1 tsp maple syrup
- 1 small clove garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp warm water
- Salt, pepper, and smoked paprika to taste

*Directions:*
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss butternut squash and broccoli with olive oil, salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Roast for 25 minutes until tender and lightly browned.
2. While vegetables roast, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, garlic, and warm water to make the dressing. Add more water if needed for a pourable consistency.
3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils and roasted vegetables.
4. Drizzle generously with the lemon-tahini dressing and toss to coat.
5. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Lentils are a fiber powerhouse — one cup provides about 16 grams of fiber, plus iron and protein. Combined with roasted vegetables, this meal supports healthy digestion and provides lasting energy through the afternoon.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Birth ball exercises are fantastic at this stage. Sitting on a birth ball (also called an exercise or yoga ball) helps open the pelvis and encourages baby to move into the optimal head-down position. Try gentle bouncing, hip circles, and figure-eight movements. Even just sitting on the ball instead of the couch while watching TV counts as beneficial.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
This week, confirm your route to the hospital. Actually drive it — at different times of day. Know where the entrance is, where you'll park, and where the labor and delivery unit is located. Some couples do a dry run at night to time it. It sounds excessive until you're driving at 2 AM with a laboring partner and realize you've never actually been to this entrance before.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What names are still on the table? Should we have our final name conversation this week?" If you haven't settled on a name yet, the pressure is officially on. If you have, maybe discuss middle name options or whether you want to share the name before birth.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At 35 weeks, your baby has already developed unique fingerprints! These formed way back around weeks 17-20, but now those little fingers are fully formed and those prints are permanent. No one else in the world — ever — will have the same ones.
