# Week 39, Day 7

## 👶 Baby Development
Baby's movements may feel different now — less dramatic kicks, more rolls and squirms. That's because there's simply less room in there. But you should still feel regular movement. If your partner notices a significant decrease in movement, don't wait — contact her healthcare provider. Kick counting is still important: 10 movements in 2 hours is the general benchmark. Baby is also continuing to practice sucking and swallowing, getting ready for that first feed.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might be experiencing a mix of "get this baby OUT of me" and "wait, I'm not ready." That contradiction is completely normal. Physically, she may be having more frequent Braxton Hicks, lower back pain, loose stools (the body's way of clearing out before labor), and trouble sleeping. Some women notice a burst of energy a day or two before labor — the legendary "nesting burst." If she suddenly wants to reorganize the entire house at midnight, labor might not be far off.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Keep the fridge stocked with easy grab-and-go foods for the coming days: pre-washed fruit, hummus and pita, yogurt cups, hard-boiled eggs, and pre-made sandwiches. When labor starts, appetite usually drops, but having light, easy foods available means neither of you will be running on empty. Also, freeze some smoothie packs — banana, berries, spinach, and protein powder in bags. Just dump in a blender with milk and you've got a nutritious meal in 30 seconds.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Gentle hip-opening movements remain beneficial. Your partner can try the "side-lying release" — lying on her side on the edge of a bed or couch, letting the top leg hang off gently while relaxing for 2-3 minutes, then switching sides. This can help release tension in the pelvic floor and round ligaments. It's also a technique used during labor to help baby descend, so practicing now means it'll feel familiar later.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Let's talk about what happens right after birth — the "Golden Hour." Ideally, within the first hour of life, baby is placed directly on mom's bare chest for skin-to-skin contact. This stabilizes baby's heart rate and temperature, jumpstarts breastfeeding, and floods both mom and baby with oxytocin. Your job during this hour: protect the space. Politely ask visitors to wait, dim the lights if possible, keep things calm, and take photos (but be present too). If a C-section happens, skin-to-skin can often still happen in the recovery room — advocate for it.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one thing about you that you hope our baby inherits?" It can be a physical trait, a personality quirk, a talent, or a value. Share yours too. It's a sweet way to imagine who this little person might become.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
A newborn's stomach is only the size of a cherry on day one, a walnut by day three, and an apricot by one week. That's why newborns eat so frequently — those tiny stomachs can't hold much! Colostrum, the first form of breast milk, is produced in exactly the right small quantities for these mini meals.

## 🔮 After-Birth Preview: The First 24 Hours
Here's a preview of what the first day with your newborn looks like: after the Golden Hour, the medical team will do newborn assessments (weight, length, Apgar scores, vitamin K shot, eye ointment). Baby might be surprisingly alert for the first hour or two, then sleep deeply for several hours. Your partner will be monitored for bleeding and vitals. If she had an epidural, feeling in her legs will gradually return. Nurses will check on the uterus regularly (not the most comfortable thing). You'll both be exhausted but running on adrenaline and love. Expect to feel overwhelmed, elated, terrified, and grateful — sometimes all at once.

## 🙏 Rest & Gratitude
Today is for rest. Both of you. Your partner is about to undertake something physically extraordinary, and you're about to be her anchor through it all. Take a nap. Watch something that makes you laugh. Order food. Hold hands. This is one of the last quiet weekends as a couple — not the last good one, but the last quiet one. Soak it in.
