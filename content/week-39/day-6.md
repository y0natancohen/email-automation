# Week 39, Day 7

## 🎉 Fun Fact
A newborn's stomach is only the size of a cherry on day one, a walnut by day three, and an apricot by one week. That's why newborns eat so frequently — those tiny stomachs can't hold much. Colostrum, the first form of breast milk, is produced in exactly the right small quantities for these mini meals.

## 👶 Baby Development
Baby's movements may feel different now — less dramatic kicks, more rolls and squirms — simply because there's less room. But regular movement should still continue. If the Mom notices a significant decrease, don't wait — contact her healthcare provider. The general benchmark is 10 movements in 2 hours. Baby is also continuing to practice sucking and swallowing, getting ready for that first feed.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom might be caught between "get this baby OUT" and "wait, I'm not ready" — and that contradiction is completely normal. Physically, more frequent Braxton Hicks, lower back pain, and loose stools (the body's way of clearing out before labor) are common. Some women notice a burst of energy a day or two before labor — if she suddenly wants to reorganize the entire house at midnight, labor might not be far off.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Keep the fridge stocked with easy grab-and-go foods for the coming days: pre-washed fruit, hummus and pita, yogurt cups, hard-boiled eggs, and pre-made sandwiches. Also consider freezing some smoothie packs — banana, berries, spinach, and protein powder in bags. Just dump into a blender with milk and you have a nutritious meal in 30 seconds.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Gentle hip-opening movements remain beneficial. The Mom can try the "side-lying release" — lying on her side on the edge of a bed, letting the top leg hang off gently while relaxing for 2-3 minutes, then switching sides. This can release tension in the pelvic floor and round ligaments. It's also a technique used during labor to help baby descend, so practicing now means it'll feel familiar later.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
The "Golden Hour" — the first hour after birth — is prime time for uninterrupted skin-to-skin contact. Baby is placed directly on the Mom's bare chest, which stabilizes heart rate and temperature, jumpstarts breastfeeding, and floods both of them with oxytocin. Your job during this hour: protect the space. Politely ask visitors to wait, dim the lights if possible, keep things calm, and take photos while staying present. If a C-section happens, skin-to-skin can often still happen in the recovery room — advocate for it.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's one thing about you that you hope our baby inherits?" It can be a physical trait, a personality quirk, a talent, or a value. Share yours too — it's a sweet way to imagine who this little person might become.

## 🔮 After-Birth Preview: The First 24 Hours
After the Golden Hour, the medical team will do newborn assessments — weight, length, Apgar scores, vitamin K shot, eye ointment. Baby might be surprisingly alert for the first hour or two, then sleep deeply for several hours. The Mom will be monitored for bleeding and vitals. Expect to feel overwhelmed, elated, terrified, and grateful — sometimes all at once.

## 🙏 Rest & Gratitude
Today is for rest. Both of you. The Mom is about to undertake something physically extraordinary, and you're about to be her anchor through it all. Take a nap, watch something that makes you laugh, order food, hold hands. This is one of the last quiet weekends as a couple — not the last good one, but the last quiet one.
