# Week 39, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to week 39 — your baby is officially FULL TERM! This is the real deal. Your little one is about 20 inches long and weighs around 7 pounds, roughly the size of a small watermelon. All of baby's organs are developed and ready for life on the outside, though that incredible brain will keep growing rapidly well after birth. Baby is shedding the last of the lanugo (that fine body hair) and the vernix (the waxy coating), swallowing it along with amniotic fluid — all of which will form part of their very first poop (meconium). Your partner's cervix may already be softening and thinning, and Braxton Hicks contractions might be getting more convincing. Every day from here on out, you could get "the call." Deep breath — you've got this.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's brain is developing at a staggering pace — it's grown about 30% in just the last few weeks and will continue this rapid growth for the first few years of life. The fat layer under baby's skin is now thick enough to help regulate body temperature after birth, giving them those adorable chubby cheeks. Lungs are fully mature and producing surfactant, the substance that keeps the tiny air sacs open. Baby's immune system is getting a final boost of antibodies from the placenta — a parting gift from mom that will help protect them in those first vulnerable weeks.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner is probably feeling very, very done. And that's completely normal. The pelvic pressure can be intense as baby drops lower into position. She might notice increased vaginal discharge or even lose her mucus plug — a glob of thick mucus that can be clear, pink, or slightly bloody. Don't panic if she mentions it; it's a sign her body is preparing, though labor could still be days or even a couple of weeks away. Sleep is likely terrible — between bathroom trips, heartburn, and the sheer difficulty of finding a comfortable position with a watermelon strapped to her front.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Time for an energy-boosting meal that's also great for late pregnancy: **One-Pan Salmon with Sweet Potato and Broccoli**. Toss cubed sweet potato and broccoli florets with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a sheet pan. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 15 minutes. Add seasoned salmon fillets (lemon, garlic, dill) and roast for another 12-15 minutes. The omega-3s from salmon support baby's brain development right up to delivery, the sweet potato provides sustained energy, and the broccoli delivers a dose of vitamin C and folate. Easy enough for you to make while your partner puts her feet up.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Walking is still the gold standard right now, and it may even help encourage baby to settle deeper into the pelvis. Encourage 15-20 minute gentle walks, stopping whenever needed. Pelvic tilts on all fours can help relieve back pressure and may even help baby get into an optimal head-down position. Remind your partner that this isn't about fitness anymore — it's about comfort and gentle movement to keep her body feeling as good as possible.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
This week, do a final run-through of your birth plan logistics. Can you get to the hospital or birthing center within a reasonable time from home? From work? Do you know alternate routes in case of traffic? Is the car seat installed? (Hospitals won't let you leave without one.) Is your phone charged? Do you have the hospital's number saved? These small practical steps will give you — and your partner — real peace of mind.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
Ask your partner: "When we're in the delivery room and things get intense, what words or phrases would feel most encouraging to you?" Some women want to hear "you're doing amazing," others prefer simple reminders to breathe, and some want total silence. Knowing her preference now means you can show up exactly the way she needs.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Only about 4% of babies arrive on their actual due date! The "due date" is really more of a "due window." Most first-time babies arrive between 39 and 41 weeks, so your baby is right on schedule — whenever they decide to show up.
