# Week 13, Day 7

## 👶 Baby Development
As week 13 wraps up, let's take stock. Your baby is the size of a peach — about 2.9 inches and 23 grams — with fingerprints, vocal cords, and the beginnings of facial expressions. The intestines are fully inside the body, and the digestive system is practicing. The immune system is building. The bones are hardening. The muscles are strengthening. Hair follicles are in place. And the placenta is running the show — a sophisticated organ that nourishes, protects, and sustains. Your baby has transitioned from a tiny, fragile embryo into a robust, active little person with a unique identity already forming.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Many women describe week 13 as a turning point. The first trimester exhaustion is fading, the nausea is lifting (or at least becoming manageable), and the pregnancy feels more settled. She might be sleeping better, eating better, and feeling more optimistic. This is also when many women start to "show" — a gentle rounding of the lower abdomen that makes the pregnancy visible. For first-time mothers, showing often happens a bit later (weeks 14-16), but every body is different. If she's not showing yet, she will soon.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Weekend Power Pancakes (Full Recipe)**

A hearty weekend breakfast to fuel the second trimester.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup whole wheat flour
- 1 ripe banana, mashed
- 1 egg
- 3/4 cup milk
- 1 tbsp ground flaxseed
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- Toppings: fresh berries, maple syrup, nut butter

**Steps:**
1. Mash banana in a bowl. Add egg and milk, whisk together.
2. Add flour, flaxseed, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Stir until just combined (lumpy is fine).
3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add a small amount of butter or cooking spray.
4. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on surface, flip, cook 1-2 more minutes.
5. Stack and top with berries and a drizzle of maple syrup.

The whole wheat flour provides fiber, the banana adds potassium and natural sweetness, the flaxseed delivers omega-3s, and the egg adds protein.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
A restful Saturday. If your partner wants to move, a gentle walk or some light stretching is perfect. But today is also a great day to prioritize rest. The body has been through a lot in the first trimester, and the second trimester will bring its own demands — growing belly, shifting center of gravity, increased weight. ## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's sense of touch is now one of the most developed senses. The entire body surface is becoming sensitive to tactile stimulation, with the most sensitive areas being the palms, soles, and face. Touch is the first sense to develop in human embryos and remains one of the most important senses throughout life. Skin-to-skin contact after birth triggers a cascade of hormonal responses in both parent and baby — including the release of oxytocin, which promotes bonding, and the stabilization of the baby's heart rate, temperature, and breathing.

## 🍼 After-Birth Preview: Postpartum Recovery
Nobody talks about this enough, so let's start now: postpartum recovery is real, and it takes time. Here's what to expect for your partner:
- **Physical recovery** from a vaginal delivery typically takes 4-6 weeks. Cesarean recovery takes 6-8 weeks.
- **Bleeding** (lochia) occurs for 2-6 weeks after birth, regardless of delivery method.
- **Hormonal shifts** after delivery are massive — progesterone and estrogen drop dramatically, which can affect mood.
- **"Baby blues"** (tearfulness, mood swings) are normal and affect up to 80% of new mothers in the first two weeks.
- **Postpartum depression** is different — more persistent, more severe, and requires professional support. It affects about 10-15% of new mothers.
- **Your role** is critical: manage the household, bring her food and water, let her sleep, and watch for signs that she needs more support.

This preview isn't meant to scare — it's meant to prepare. Prepared partners make better partners.

## 🔮 Next Week Preview
Week 14 brings more exciting developments. Your baby will be the size of a lemon and can squint, frown, and grimace. The roof of the mouth will be fully formed. Your partner's belly will likely become more noticeable, and the second trimester energy boost should be in full swing.
