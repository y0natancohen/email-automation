# Week 13, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Here's something remarkable: your baby is starting to make facial expressions. The muscles in the face are developing and connecting to the brain, allowing your baby to squint, grimace, and make mouth movements. These aren't emotional expressions yet — they're reflexive, like practice runs. But they mean the facial muscles and the nerves that control them are wiring up correctly. On a detailed ultrasound, you might catch your baby frowning, puckering, or opening its mouth wide. Meanwhile, the head is now about one-third of the total body length (down from one-half a few weeks ago), and the body is catching up fast.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The belly is likely becoming more noticeable, especially to your partner herself. She might be transitioning into maternity clothes — or at least into stretchy waistbands. The transition can feel awkward: she might not feel "pregnant enough" for maternity wear but too big for her regular wardrobe. A few versatile pieces — stretchy leggings, flowy tops, belly bands that extend the life of regular pants — can bridge the gap. She might also notice increased appetite, particularly for specific foods. Cravings in the second trimester tend to be more pleasant than first-trimester aversions.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**One-Pan Roasted Veggies and Chicken Thighs (Full Recipe)**

Easy, nutritious, and minimal cleanup.

**Ingredients:**
- 4 chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on)
- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 1 sweet potato, cubed
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 zucchini, sliced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp paprika, 1 tsp oregano, salt and pepper
- Lemon wedges for serving

**Steps:**
1. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
2. Toss vegetables with 2 tbsp olive oil, garlic, half the spices, and salt.
3. Rub chicken thighs with remaining olive oil and spices.
4. Spread vegetables on a large baking sheet. Nestle chicken thighs on top.
5. Roast for 35-40 minutes until chicken is golden and veggies are tender.
6. Squeeze lemon over everything before serving.

Chicken thighs are richer in iron and zinc than breast meat, and they stay moist and flavorful.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Yoga: Goddess Pose (Utkata Konasana)**
Stand with feet wide apart (about 3 feet), toes turned out at 45 degrees. Bend the knees deeply, sinking the hips toward the floor. Bring hands together at the chest in prayer position. Hold for 30 seconds. Rise up, rest, repeat 3 times. This pose strengthens the legs, opens the hips, and builds stamina — all useful for labor. Add arm variations: extend arms overhead, or open them wide like wings for an added shoulder stretch.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
If your partner is shopping for maternity clothes, go with her — or at least be enthusiastic and supportive when she shows you what she's found. This transition can be emotionally loaded. She might feel like she's "giving up" her normal wardrobe, or she might feel awkward in maternity wear. Your genuine, specific compliments ("that color looks amazing on you" or "you look really comfortable in that") go a long way. Avoid: "you don't even look pregnant yet" — even if meant as a compliment, it can feel dismissive.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's intestines are now fully settled inside the abdomen, and they've started producing meconium — the dark, tarry substance that will become your baby's first bowel movement after birth. Meconium is made up of amniotic fluid, mucus, skin cells, bile, and lanugo (fine hair) that the baby swallows. It's sterile and odorless, and passing it after birth is a sign that the digestive system is working properly. Fair warning: meconium is impressively sticky and dark. It's a rite of passage for new parents.

## 😴 Sleep Tip
As your partner's belly grows, finding a comfortable sleep position becomes an ongoing project. Here's a configuration worth trying: lie on the left side (optimal for blood flow), place a pillow between the knees (aligns the hips), tuck a thin pillow under the belly (supports the weight), and optionally place a pillow behind the back (prevents rolling onto the back). This sounds like a pillow fortress, and it is. But many women swear by it. If buying a dedicated pregnancy pillow, the C-shape is the most popular and doesn't dominate the entire bed.

## 💼 Workplace Pregnancy Tip
If your partner has announced at work, she might start getting unsolicited advice and stories from coworkers — some helpful, some not. A useful boundary phrase: "Thank you for sharing — I'll definitely look into that." It acknowledges the person without committing to anything. She doesn't owe anyone details about her birth plan, feeding choices, or medical decisions. She can share what she wants and keep the rest private. Setting these boundaries early makes the remaining months at work much more manageable.
