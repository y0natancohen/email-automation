# Week 13, Day 4

## 🌳 Baby Development
A major behind-the-scenes transition has completed: the placenta has fully taken over hormone production from the corpus luteum. This is significant because the placenta is a much more stable hormone factory — which is why many women start feeling better around this time. Your baby is about 2.9 inches long and growing fast. The body is lengthening, the limbs are proportional, and the movements are becoming more coordinated. Your baby can now turn its head, stretch its arms, and flex its tiny feet.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby can now respond to touch. If you gently poke the Mom's belly, the baby might wriggle away — though she won't feel the movement yet. This shows that the nervous system is developing tactile awareness. Nerve endings are spreading throughout the body, and by the third trimester, your baby will respond not just to touch but to sound, light, and even temperature changes.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
With the placenta running the hormonal show, the Mom might notice more emotional stability. The wild mood swings of the first trimester may begin to mellow. She might also notice the linea nigra appearing — a dark vertical line from the pubic bone toward the belly button, caused by increased melanin production. It's more prominent in women with darker skin tones and fades after delivery. Some women also notice skin tags in areas of friction, which are harmless and usually resolve postpartum.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
The Mom needs about 71 grams of protein per day during pregnancy — significantly more than the usual 46 grams. A day of protein-rich eating might look like: two eggs and yogurt for breakfast (22g), chicken breast in a salad for lunch (25g), almonds and a cheese stick for a snack (12g), and grilled fish with quinoa for dinner (30g). Vegetarian? Tofu, lentils, chickpeas, tempeh, and dairy can easily meet the target.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
A resistance band workout takes 15 minutes and is one of the most versatile pregnancy fitness tools. Try banded squats (band above knees), pull-aparts at chest height, banded side steps (band around ankles), overhead tricep extensions, and standing rows (stepping on the band). Three sets of 10-15 reps for each.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
The Mom's emotional landscape is changing along with her body. She might be processing her shifting identity — from individual to mother. This can bring up unexpected feelings: excitement, fear, loss of independence, overwhelming love, and everything in between. You don't need to fix these feelings. You just need to be a safe place where she can express them. Listen, validate, hold space.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
What's one thing you've always wanted to teach a child? Maybe it's fishing, playing guitar, cooking a family recipe, or speaking another language. Share your dreams of what you want to pass on.

## 🔬 Deep Dive: How Babies Learn in the Womb
Your baby isn't just growing — it's learning. Research shows that fetuses demonstrate memory and learning abilities from surprisingly early on. Babies exposed to specific sounds in the womb show recognition and preference for those sounds after birth. Flavors from food cross into amniotic fluid, and babies show preference for familiar flavors after birth. Newborns can distinguish their mother's voice from other voices within hours. The takeaway: talk to the belly, play music, share meals. It all matters.
