# Week 13, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to week 13 — the official start of the second trimester! Your baby is now the size of a peach (~2.9 inches, 7.4 cm) and weighs about 23 grams. Here's what's happening this monumental week:
- Vocal cords are forming in the larynx
- Fingerprints are beginning to develop — your baby is getting a unique identity
- Facial expressions are emerging: your baby can squint and make mouth movements
- The intestines are now fully inside the abdomen
- The placenta has taken over hormone production entirely
- Energy often starts returning for your partner
- Your baby's head is now about one-third of the body (down from one-half a few weeks ago)
- Teeth, bones, and muscles continue to develop rapidly

The second trimester is often called the "golden trimester" — less nausea, more energy, and the exciting milestones of feeling kicks and watching the belly grow. You've earned this.

## 👶 Baby Development
Fingerprints. Your baby is developing fingerprints right now — this very moment. The friction ridges on the fingertips are forming in a pattern that is 100% unique. No one who has ever lived, and no one who ever will live, will have the same fingerprints as your child. The pattern is influenced by genetics but also by the exact conditions in the womb: the position of the fingers, the flow of amniotic fluid, the speed of growth. Even identical twins develop different fingerprints. Your baby is becoming one of a kind in the most literal sense possible.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Relief. Energy. Hunger. The second trimester often feels like waking up from a long, queasy nap. Many women report a noticeable increase in energy and a decrease in nausea around this week. Your partner might start feeling more like herself — and more excited about the pregnancy. Her belly may be showing a slight curve, especially in tighter clothing. Some women "pop" suddenly during week 13-14, while others carry discreetly for several more weeks. Breast changes continue — they may have grown one or two cup sizes already.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Grilled Chicken and Mango Salad (Full Recipe)**

A light, fresh meal for the returning appetite.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 chicken breast, grilled and sliced
- 1 ripe mango, cubed
- 2 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, romaine)
- 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup roasted cashews
- Fresh mint leaves

**Dressing:**
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- 1 tsp honey
- Pinch of salt and chili flakes

**Steps:**
1. Grill chicken breast (or use leftover chicken). Slice thinly.
2. Arrange greens on a plate. Top with mango, onion, chicken, and cashews.
3. Whisk dressing ingredients together.
4. Drizzle dressing over salad. Garnish with fresh mint.

This salad provides lean protein, vitamin A (mango), iron (greens), and healthy fats (cashews, olive oil).

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Celebrate the second trimester with a slightly longer walk — 25-30 minutes if your partner is feeling up to it. This is often the week where exercise starts to feel good again, rather than like an obligation. If she was active before pregnancy, she might feel ready to return to modified versions of her regular workouts. Key guidelines from ACOG: aim for 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, avoid exercises with fall risk, skip anything that involves lying flat on the back for extended periods (after the first trimester), and always stay hydrated.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Now that the second trimester has begun, start thinking about a "babymoon" — a trip or getaway just for the two of you before the baby arrives. The second trimester (especially weeks 14-28) is the ideal time for travel: energy is up, nausea is down, and the belly isn't yet unwieldy. It doesn't have to be elaborate — a weekend at the Dead Sea, a cabin in the Galilee, or even a nice hotel in a nearby city. The point is dedicated couple time.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"How should we handle unsolicited advice from family and friends? What's our strategy?" Because it's coming — belly-touching, birth stories, parenting opinions — and having a game plan helps you navigate it as a team rather than individually.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's vocal cords are forming this week, but the baby won't make a sound until birth. Why? Sound requires air, and the baby's lungs are filled with amniotic fluid. The first cry at birth happens when the lungs inflate with air for the first time and push it past the vocal cords. That cry is actually a critical sign that the respiratory system is working. Before birth, babies do practice "crying" movements — their mouths open, their chests move — but no sound comes out. It's a silent rehearsal for the big debut.

## 👫 Couple Activity
**"Letter to Our Future Selves":** Each of you write a letter to yourselves one year from today. Write about what you're feeling right now, what you're worried about, what you're excited about, and what you hope your life looks like in a year. Seal the letters. Open them together in one year, when your baby is about 5 months old. You'll be amazed at how much has changed — and how much of what worried you turned out fine.
