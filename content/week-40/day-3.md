# Week 40, Day 3

## 👶 Baby Development
Fun little detail: your baby might already need a nail trim at birth. Those tiny fingernails have grown past the fingertips, and some babies are born with small scratches on their face from their own nails in the womb. Baby's hair situation is anyone's guess — some are born with a full head of hair, others are completely bald, and it has nothing to do with the heartburn your partner may have experienced (despite the old wives' tale... although one small study actually did find a correlation!).

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The due date might be today, yesterday, or tomorrow — and if baby hasn't arrived, your partner may be feeling frustrated, anxious, or like her body has "failed" somehow. It hasn't. Not even close. First babies commonly arrive after the due date, and her body is doing exactly what it's supposed to do on exactly the right timeline. The due date is an estimate based on averages — her body and baby aren't following a textbook, they're following their own perfect schedule.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Hydration is absolutely critical right now. Dehydration can actually trigger Braxton Hicks contractions and make your partner feel worse overall. She should be drinking at least 8-10 glasses of water daily. Make it easier: fill a large water bottle every morning and keep it within arm's reach. Add lemon, cucumber, or mint to make it more appealing. Coconut water is a great option too — natural electrolytes without added sugar.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
A technique called "curb walking" can help encourage baby's descent. Your partner walks with one foot on the curb and one on the street (or any slightly elevated surface and the ground beside it). The uneven stance opens the pelvis asymmetrically and can help baby rotate and drop lower. Try it for 5-10 minutes during a walk, alternating which foot is elevated. It looks a bit silly, but many midwives swear by it.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Boredom is a real risk right now. You're both on high alert, maybe you've taken time off work, and... nothing is happening. Fill the waiting time intentionally: binge a show together, cook a fancy meal (for real, when's the next time you'll have a leisurely dinner?), play cards, go for a date-night walk. Don't just sit around refreshing pregnancy apps. Live these last days as a couple to the fullest.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's something you want to make sure we still do as a couple after the baby arrives?" Date nights, morning coffee rituals, inside jokes — the things that make you "you" as partners don't have to disappear. Talking about it now makes it more likely to happen.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Babies are born with a natural "stepping reflex." If you hold a newborn upright with their feet touching a flat surface, they'll make stepping motions as if trying to walk. This reflex disappears after about two months but reappears around 12 months when they actually start learning to walk. Evolution left the instruction manual installed — it just needs a software update first.

## 🛏️ Sleep Tip
For your partner: try the "progressive muscle relaxation" technique tonight. Starting from her toes, she tenses each muscle group for 5 seconds, then releases. Move up through the calves, thighs, glutes, hands, arms, shoulders, and face. The release after tension signals the body to relax deeply. Combined with slow breathing, this can help ease the physical tension that's making sleep so difficult. For you: consider sleeping in shifts if she's up frequently. You don't both need to be awake at 3 AM... yet.

## 💼 Workplace Tip
If you're still working, have the "I might disappear at any moment" conversation with your team if you haven't already. Set clear expectations: your out-of-office will be on within minutes of getting the call, and you've already briefed your backup person. Having a one-page handoff document ready (key contacts, deadlines, project status) means you can walk out the door guilt-free when the time comes. Also, some employers are flexible about starting paternity leave early if you're past the due date and the waiting is affecting your focus — it doesn't hurt to ask.
