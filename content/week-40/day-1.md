# Week 40, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
This is it — week 40, due date week! Your baby is about 20 inches long and weighs approximately 7.5 pounds, about the size of a watermelon. And yet, only about 4% of babies actually arrive on their due date, so try to think of this week as more of a "due zone" than a deadline. Baby's skull bones are designed to be slightly flexible and unfused so they can mold through the birth canal. The placenta is still actively delivering antibodies, giving baby an immune system starter kit. Your partner may be experiencing everything from renewed energy to complete exhaustion, Braxton Hicks that feel suspiciously real, and an emotional pendulum swinging between "let's DO this" and "can we postpone?" The finish line is right there — even if you can't see it yet.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is producing cortisol — not the stress kind, but the life-saving kind. This cortisol surge helps the lungs produce enough surfactant for independent breathing after birth. Baby's skin is now smooth and soft, though it might still have patches of vernix in the creases. The lanugo is almost entirely gone. Baby's intestines are full of meconium, and those first diaper changes are going to be... memorable. Your little one is fully ready for the outside world — they're just waiting for their cue.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might be oscillating between intense impatience and genuine fear, and both are valid. Physically, she may notice the "bloody show" — a small amount of blood-tinged mucus that signals the cervix is changing. This can happen days before labor or right as it begins. She might also feel increased pelvic pressure, more frequent trips to the bathroom, and lower back pain that doesn't go away. If she's feeling a sudden urge to clean, organize, or "prepare" — that nesting instinct is evolution's way of getting her ready.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Labor Day Energy Bites:** Mix 1 cup oats, 1/2 cup peanut butter, 1/3 cup honey, 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips, 1/4 cup ground flaxseed, and 1 tsp vanilla. Roll into balls and refrigerate. These are perfect for early labor snacking, hospital bag treats, and postpartum fuel. High in protein, healthy fats, and slow-release carbs — everything you both need for energy. Make a double batch and keep some in the freezer.

**Raspberry Leaf Tea & Date Smoothie (Labor-Prep Power Drink)**

Raspberry leaf tea has been used by midwives for centuries to help tone the uterus and prepare for labor. Combined with dates (linked to easier labor in studies), this smoothie is a delicious way to get both in one glass.

*Ingredients:*
- 1 cup brewed raspberry leaf tea, cooled
- 4 Medjool dates, pitted
- 1/2 cup frozen mango chunks
- 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 1 tbsp ground flaxseed
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Handful of ice

*Directions:*
1. Brew raspberry leaf tea (1 tea bag in 1 cup boiling water for 10 minutes). Let it cool to room temperature, or make it the night before and refrigerate.
2. Add cooled tea, dates, mango, yogurt, flaxseed, cinnamon, and ice to a blender.
3. Blend on high until completely smooth — dates may take an extra 15 seconds to break down fully.
4. Pour into a tall glass and sip slowly.

Raspberry leaf tea is caffeine-free and rich in minerals like iron and calcium. The dates provide natural sweetness plus the labor-prep benefits, while the yogurt and flaxseed add protein and omega-3s. A nourishing ritual for due date week.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Walking remains the best thing your partner can do right now. It helps baby engage deeper in the pelvis, and gravity is a natural ally. Aim for short, easy walks — even 10 minutes around the block counts. If she feels up to it, walking up stairs (slowly, with a handrail) can help open the pelvis even more. The key is gentle, consistent movement, not intensity.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's your due date week mindset: be ready, but don't hover. Your partner doesn't need you asking "how are you feeling?" every 20 minutes (even though you want to). Instead, show your readiness through actions — keep the car gassed up, your phone charged, and your bag by the door. Then go about your day calmly. Your calm energy is contagious, and right now, that's the best thing you can give her.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's the first thing you want to eat after the baby is born?" It sounds silly, but after hours of labor (and possibly restricted eating), that first post-birth meal is legendary. Some moms dream about it for weeks. Plan it now — whether it's pizza delivery to the hospital room or her favorite takeout.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At birth, your baby will have approximately 10,000 taste buds — far more than adults, who have about 5,000. They're located not just on the tongue but also on the roof, sides, and back of the mouth. Newborns have a strong preference for sweet tastes, which makes biological sense since breast milk is naturally sweet.

## 👫 Couple Activity
Write a letter to your baby together — or separately, and then read them to each other. It doesn't need to be long or fancy. Tell your baby what this wait has been like, what you're feeling right now, what you hope for them. Seal it and date it. Give it to them on their 18th birthday, or their wedding day, or whenever feels right. The words you write tonight, full of anticipation and love, will be priceless someday.
