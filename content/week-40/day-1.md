# Week 40, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
This is it — week 40, due date week. Your baby is about 20 inches long and weighs approximately 7.5 pounds, about the size of a watermelon. And yet, only about 4% of babies actually arrive on their due date, so think of this as a "due zone" rather than a deadline. Baby's skull bones are designed to be slightly flexible and unfused so they can mold through the birth canal, and the placenta is still actively delivering antibodies as an immune system starter kit. The finish line is right there — even if you can't quite see it yet.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At birth, your baby will have approximately 10,000 taste buds — far more than adults, who have about 5,000. They're located not just on the tongue but also on the roof, sides, and back of the mouth. Newborns have a strong preference for sweet tastes, which makes biological sense since breast milk is naturally sweet.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby is producing cortisol — not the stress kind, but the life-saving kind. This cortisol surge helps the lungs produce enough surfactant for independent breathing. Baby's skin is smooth and soft, with perhaps some remaining patches of vernix in the creases. The lanugo is almost entirely gone. The intestines are full of meconium, and those first diaper changes will be memorable. Your little one is fully ready for the outside world — just waiting for their cue.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The Mom might be oscillating between intense impatience and genuine fear, and both are valid. She may notice the "bloody show" — a small amount of blood-tinged mucus that signals the cervix is changing, which can happen days before labor or right as it begins. Increased pelvic pressure, more frequent bathroom trips, and persistent lower back pain are all common. If she's feeling a sudden urge to clean and organize, that nesting instinct is evolution's way of getting her ready.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Quick energy bites for the hospital bag and beyond: mix oats, peanut butter, honey, dark chocolate chips, ground flaxseed, and vanilla. Roll into balls and refrigerate. High in protein, healthy fats, and slow-release carbs — everything you both need for sustained energy. Make a double batch and keep some in the freezer.

Raspberry leaf tea has been used by midwives for centuries to help tone the uterus. Combined with dates (linked to easier labor in studies), a smoothie with cooled raspberry leaf tea, pitted dates, frozen mango, Greek yogurt, flaxseed, and cinnamon makes a nourishing ritual for due date week. The tea is caffeine-free and rich in iron and calcium.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Walking remains the best thing the Mom can do right now. It helps baby engage deeper in the pelvis, and gravity is a natural ally. Aim for short, easy walks — even 10 minutes around the block counts. If she feels up to it, walking up stairs slowly with a handrail can help open the pelvis further. The key is gentle, consistent movement, not intensity.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Here's your due date week mindset: be ready, but don't hover. The Mom doesn't need you asking "how are you feeling?" every 20 minutes. Instead, show your readiness through actions — keep the car gassed up, your phone charged, and your bag by the door. Then go about your day calmly. Your calm energy is contagious, and right now, that's the best thing you can give her.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What's the first thing you want to eat after the baby is born?" After hours of labor (and possibly restricted eating), that first post-birth meal is legendary. Some moms dream about it for weeks. Plan it now.

## 👫 Couple Activity
Write a letter to your baby together — or separately, and then read them to each other. Tell your baby what this wait has been like, what you're feeling right now, what you hope for them. Seal it, date it, and give it to them someday. The words you write tonight, full of anticipation and love, will be priceless.
