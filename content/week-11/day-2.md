# Week 11, Day 2

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby's nose is starting to work — sort of. The nasal passages are opening at the tip of the nose, even though they won't be used for breathing until birth. The bridge of the nose is becoming more defined, and the nostrils are visible. This gives your baby's face a much more human appearance. Meanwhile, the tongue and palate (roof of the mouth) are forming, which will eventually allow your baby to eat, speak, and make faces at you. The irises of the eyes are developing, though the final eye color won't be determined until months after birth.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
Your partner might notice her hair looking fuller and more lustrous. This isn't because new hair is growing faster — it's because pregnancy hormones slow down the normal hair shedding cycle. She's losing fewer hairs per day, so the overall volume increases. (Fair warning: after delivery, all that retained hair tends to fall out at once, usually around 3-4 months postpartum. It's alarming but temporary.) She might also notice her nails growing faster and stronger — or more brittle. Hormones are unpredictable.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
Quick tip: B6 (pyridoxine) can help with nausea. Foods rich in B6 include bananas, chickpeas, potatoes, poultry, and sunflower seeds. If food isn't cutting it, some OBs recommend a B6 supplement (typically 25 mg, three times a day) — but always check with the doctor first. A simple B6-rich snack: banana slices with sunflower seed butter on whole grain toast.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
**Prenatal Pilates Basics (15 minutes):**
1. **Pelvic Tilts** — Lie on your back (still safe at this stage), knees bent, feet flat. Gently tilt the pelvis upward, flattening the lower back against the floor. Hold 5 seconds. Release. Repeat 15 times.
2. **Leg Slides** — Same position. Slowly slide one leg out straight along the floor, then slide it back. Alternate legs. 10 per side. Strengthens the deep core.
3. **Bridges** — Feet flat, lift hips off the floor into a bridge. Hold for 5 seconds. Lower slowly. 10 reps. Great for glutes and pelvic stability.
4. **Side-Lying Leg Lifts** — Lie on your side, lift the top leg about 12 inches, lower slowly. 15 per side. Targets hip stabilizers.

These exercises build core stability and strength, which directly supports the spine as the belly grows.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
Start researching pediatricians (rofe yeladim) now. You don't need to decide immediately, but it helps to know your options. Ask friends with kids for recommendations. Consider proximity to home, availability for urgent visits, and whether the doctor's communication style matches yours. Some kupot cholim let you choose specific pediatricians; others assign by clinic. Understanding the system now saves scrambling later.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At 11 weeks, your baby already has a unique blood type, and blood is circulating through a network of tiny blood vessels. Here's what's wild: the blood vessels in the placenta, placed end to end, would stretch about 200 miles. That's roughly the distance from Tel Aviv to Eilat. All of that vasculature exists to deliver oxygen and nutrients to a baby the size of a fig.

## 💡 Yesterday's Q&A Answer
Yesterday we asked: is hair color determined at conception, or can it change? The answer: **both!** The genes that determine hair color are set at conception, but the expression of those genes can change over time. Many babies are born with dark hair that falls out and is replaced by a completely different color. And plenty of people who were blonde as toddlers become brunette as adults. Hair color is controlled by multiple genes and the amount and type of melanin produced, which can shift throughout life.

## 🏋️ Exercise Focus: Why Posture Matters Now
As the uterus grows and the center of gravity shifts, posture becomes increasingly important. Poor posture during pregnancy can lead to back pain, neck tension, and headaches. A few tips:
- **Standing:** Imagine a string pulling the crown of your head toward the ceiling. Shoulders back and down, not hunched forward.
- **Sitting:** Feet flat on the floor, back supported. A small lumbar pillow can make a huge difference.
- **Sleeping:** A pillow between the knees keeps the spine aligned.
- **Lifting:** Always bend at the knees, not the waist. Use leg muscles, not the back.

Your partner's posture habits now will affect how she feels for the rest of the pregnancy.
