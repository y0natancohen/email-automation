## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Weekend Banana Oat Muffins (Full Recipe)**

Batch-bake these for the week ahead. Perfect for quick breakfasts and snacks.

**Ingredients:**
- 2 ripe bananas, mashed
- 2 cups rolled oats
- 2 eggs
- 1/3 cup Greek yogurt
- 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- Optional add-ins: chocolate chips, walnuts, blueberries

**Steps:**
1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
2. Blend oats in a food processor until they resemble flour (or use oat flour).
3. In a bowl, mix mashed bananas, eggs, yogurt, honey, vanilla, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.
4. Add oat flour and mix until just combined. Fold in optional add-ins.
5. Divide batter among 12 muffin cups.
6. Bake for 18-22 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
7. Cool on a wire rack. Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days or freeze for later.

No refined flour, no added oil. Just whole food ingredients packed with fiber, potassium, protein, and natural sweetness.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
At 14 weeks, your baby has approximately 60-80 milliliters of amniotic fluid surrounding it. That number will keep increasing until about week 36, when it peaks at around 800-1000 ml (nearly a quart). The amniotic fluid is replenished constantly — the entire volume is replaced every 3 hours. By full term, your baby will be swallowing about 500 ml of fluid per day and urinating about the same amount. This fluid environment is so critical that doctors monitor amniotic fluid levels throughout pregnancy using ultrasound.

## 🍼 After-Birth Preview: The Fourth Trimester
There's a concept called the "fourth trimester" — the first three months after birth. The idea is that human babies are born relatively undeveloped compared to other mammals (because of our large brains and narrow birth canals), so the first 12 weeks of life outside the womb are essentially a continuation of fetal development. During this period:
- Your baby prefers being held, rocked, and soothed — mimicking the womb environment
- Swaddling, shushing, and gentle swaying can calm a fussy baby
- Your baby doesn't have a circadian rhythm yet and won't develop day-night patterns until around 3-4 months
- Frequent feeding (every 2-3 hours) is normal and expected
- The postpartum period is intense for both parents — sleep deprivation is real

Understanding the fourth trimester now helps set realistic expectations and reduces the "what's wrong with my baby?" panic that many new parents feel.
