# Week 14, Day 1

## 🌒 This Week at a Glance
Welcome to week 14! Your baby is now the size of a lemon (~3.4 inches, 8.7 cm) and weighs about 43 grams — nearly triple the weight from just two weeks ago. Here's what's unfolding this week:
- Your baby can now squint, frown, and grimace — real facial movements
- The roof of the mouth (palate) is fully formed
- Lanugo — a fine, downy hair — is starting to cover the body
- The thyroid gland is producing hormones for the first time
- Your baby is practicing breathing movements, inhaling and exhaling amniotic fluid
- The body is growing faster than the head, bringing proportions closer to newborn ratios
- Your partner's belly is likely starting to show
- The second trimester energy boost should be noticeable now

This week feels like a shift from "early pregnancy" to "real pregnancy." Things are getting exciting.

## 👶 Baby Development
Your baby has a personality — well, sort of. Those facial muscles are now sophisticated enough to produce recognizable expressions. Your baby can squint, frown, grimace, and even make a face that looks like smiling (though true social smiling won't come until weeks after birth). These expressions are reflexive and unrelated to emotions at this stage, but they show that the complex network of nerves and muscles in the face is wiring up correctly. The roof of the mouth is now fully formed — two palatal shelves have fused together along the midline, creating the hard palate. This is an important developmental milestone, as the palate separates the mouth from the nasal cavity.

## 🤰 What Mom Might Be Feeling
The bump is arriving. For first-time mothers, week 14 is often when the belly transitions from "maybe I ate too much pasta" to "I think I might be pregnant." The uterus is now the size of a grapefruit and has risen above the pubic bone, which means the bump is becoming visible from the outside. Your partner might feel a mix of excitement (it's real! it shows!) and vulnerability (everyone can see now). Her body is changing in ways that are no longer private, and that transition can be emotionally complex. She might also feel hungrier than ever — the second trimester is when caloric needs genuinely increase.

## 🍽️ Nutrition Tip
**Baked Eggs in Avocado (Full Recipe)**

Simple, beautiful, and nutrient-dense.

**Ingredients:**
- 2 ripe avocados
- 4 eggs
- Salt, pepper, chili flakes
- Fresh herbs (chives or cilantro)
- Optional: crumbled feta, cherry tomatoes

**Steps:**
1. Preheat oven to 220°C (425°F).
2. Halve avocados and remove the pit. Scoop out a little extra flesh to make room for the egg.
3. Place avocado halves in a muffin tin or on a baking sheet (use crumpled foil to stabilize them).
4. Crack one egg into each avocado half. Season with salt, pepper, and chili flakes.
5. Bake for 12-15 minutes until the egg white is set but the yolk is still slightly soft.
6. Top with herbs and optional feta.

This gives you healthy fats (avocado), protein and choline (eggs), and a beautiful Instagram-worthy breakfast.

## 💪 Exercise & Movement
Start the week with a 25-minute walk at a brisk pace. Now that energy is returning, try to establish a consistent walking routine — ideally 5 days per week. Walking is the foundation of prenatal fitness: it's safe for all trimesters, requires no equipment, and provides cardiovascular, muscular, and mental health benefits. If the weather's good, walk outside. Fresh air and sunlight add their own benefits (vitamin D, mood regulation, improved sleep). Track your walks if it helps with motivation.

## 💡 Tip for Dad
As the belly becomes visible, your partner might receive comments from strangers and acquaintances. Some will be lovely. Some will be weird. Some will be unwanted. ("You're so big! Are you sure it's not twins?" "You're so small! Are you eating enough?") Be her buffer. If someone says something that bothers her, validate her feelings later. "That was weird. Are you okay?" goes a long way. Don't dismiss her reactions.

## 💬 Conversation Starter
"What kind of home environment do you want our child to grow up in? Quiet or lively? Structured or free-flowing? Lots of visitors or more private?" These aren't just abstract preferences — they shape real daily decisions about routines, social life, and boundaries.

## 🎉 Fun Fact
Your baby's thyroid gland — a butterfly-shaped gland in the neck — started producing thyroid hormones this week. These hormones are essential for brain development, metabolism, and growth. Until now, the baby relied entirely on thyroid hormones crossing the placenta from your partner. The fact that the baby's own thyroid is now contributing marks another step toward independence. This is also why iodine in the diet is so important — the thyroid needs iodine to produce its hormones.
